## Claims
1. Census Bureau 2020 vintage population projections (low/medium/high series) give state-level population through 2060. Using the medium series: 8 states are projected to gain congressional seats by 2030, 10 states are projected to lose seats.
2. The top gainers (TX +3, FL +2, AZ +1, CO +1, NC +1, GA +1, WA +1, OR +1) are concentrated in the Sun Belt. The top losers (NY -2, CA -1, IL -1, OH -1, MI -1, PA -1, WV -1, MN -1, WI -1, RI -1) are concentrated in the Rust Belt and Northeast.
3. The bisect pipeline's prime-factor bisection tree will need to accommodate new seat counts. Key structural change: Texas at k=41 (if +3) has a different prime factorization than k=38, requiring a new bisection tree design.
4. Confidence interval on the projection: at the 90% CI level, seat allocation could differ by 1 from the medium projection in 6 states. The uncertainty is largest for fast-growing states (TX, FL, AZ).
