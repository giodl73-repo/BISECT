## Research Question
Does the method of redistricting affect democratic outcomes — competitiveness, turnout, polarization, representation quality — beyond the partisan composition of the plan itself?

## Claims
1. Four measurable outcome dimensions exist: electoral competitiveness (O.1), voter participation (O.2), legislative behavior (O.3), and geographic representation quality (O.4).
2. Prior literature conflates redistricting quality with partisan outcome; this track separates them by controlling for partisanship and measuring democratic process quality independently.
3. Compact algorithmic plans predict better outcomes on all four dimensions compared to partisan gerrymanders, independent of which party benefits from the gerrymander.

## Framework
Outcome = f(plan_type, partisanship, incumbency, national_tide, state_fixed_effects)
Plan_type ∈ {partisan_gerrymander, bipartisan_incumbent_protection, commission, algorithmic}
The track's identifying variation: within-state changes in plan type across redistricting cycles.
