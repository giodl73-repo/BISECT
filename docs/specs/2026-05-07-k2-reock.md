# Reock Score in Algorithmic Redistricting

**Series**: K.2
**Status**: Reviewed draft; implementation-boundary pass completed 2026-05-13
**Target**: Political Analysis

## Algorithm / Subject

Empirical study of Reock-related compactness scores across bisect structure algorithms. Covers the canonical formula (area divided by exact minimum enclosing circle area), the current `bisect-analysis` centroid-plus-maximum-boundary-radius reported metric, the moving-knife point-cloud proxy, boundary sensitivity relative to PP, and quantification of the reported-Reock--PP gap across NC, WI, and TX. The implementation-boundary pass completed on 2026-05-13 explicitly separates exact-MBC Reock from the production proxy.

## Key Claims

1. The reported Reock proxy is less sensitive than PP to jagged coastline and riverine boundaries in the NC fixture: it decreases by $\leq 0.02$ when switching from smoothed to raw TIGER/Line boundaries, versus PP decreasing by $0.04$–$0.08$.
2. The moving-knife algorithm uses projected tract centroids, directional prefix cuts, and a point-cloud enclosing-circle proxy; it does not maximise exact polygon-MBC Reock by construction.
3. The empirical reported-Reock--PP gap averages about $+0.12$ to $+0.15$ across the cited fixtures, showing metric disagreement in those rows, not a universal theorem that Reock is more lenient than PP.

## Test Invariants

- L0: canonical exact-MBC Reock and the production centroid-radius proxy both remain in $[0, 1]$ on valid non-degenerate geometries; the centroid-radius proxy is disclosed as an approximation.
- L1: on simple square/rectangle fixtures, reported Reock values match the implemented formula within tolerance.
- L2: NC 2020 moving-knife mean reported Reock $>$ standard-bisect mean reported Reock in the fixture set; this is implementation evidence, not proof of exact-MBC optimality.

## Legal / Practitioner Value

Reock score has been cited in redistricting litigation in North Carolina (Harper v. Hall), Wisconsin (Gill v. Whitford), Georgia, and Florida. Reock is useful when districts include coastal or riverine boundaries because it is less sensitive to boundary irregularities than PP, but expert reports should disclose whether values are exact-MBC Reock or the BISECT centroid-radius proxy. This paper provides reported Reock values for bisect plans and documents the MKA compactness-search proxy for expert-report context, not as a legal-certification guarantee.
