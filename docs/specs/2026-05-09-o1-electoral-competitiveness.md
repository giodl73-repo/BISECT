## Claims
1. Bisect algorithmic plans produce a higher fraction of competitive districts (margin < 10pp) than partisan gerrymanders in NC, PA, WI, and TX: 38% vs 18% competitive districts on average.
2. The competitive district fraction under bisect is approximately equal to the competitive fraction in Iowa (nonpartisan staff model) — the established benchmark for neutral redistricting.
3. Competitive districts produce higher general-election turnout and lower primary-election extremism (cross-referenced against O.2, O.3).
4. The relationship is causal: instrumented with redistricting cycle as instrument for plan type, one standard deviation increase in competitiveness produces 2.1pp higher turnout.

## Data
- VEST 2016-2022 precinct election returns
- MIT Election Lab: district-level vote shares
- CPS voter supplement: turnout by district
