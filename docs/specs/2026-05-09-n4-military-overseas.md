## Research Question

Military personnel stationed overseas and their dependents are counted at their "home of record" for redistricting. This creates large population spikes in military-heavy states (VA, NC, TX, CA, WA). How does this affect bisect's district drawing, and what is the legal basis?

## Claims

1. Approximately 200,000 overseas military members and 400,000 overseas dependents are allocated to home-of-record states. Virginia (Fort Belvoir, Pentagon) and North Carolina (Ft. Bragg, Camp Lejeune) have the largest concentrations.
2. The military population in Fayetteville NC inflates the 9th congressional district by ~8% relative to the civilian population alone.
3. Bisect draws districts based on Census total population, which includes military at home-of-record. A `--balance-metric civilian_only` option would exclude them.
4. Military voters skew Republican; inflating military-heavy districts therefore has a mild Republican-advantaging effect in affected areas.
