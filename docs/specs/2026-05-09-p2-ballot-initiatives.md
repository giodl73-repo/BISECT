## Claims
1. Three states have successfully enacted redistricting reform through ballot initiative: California (Prop 11, 2008; Prop 20, 2010), Colorado (Amendments Y/Z, 2018), Michigan (Prop 2, 2018). All three bypass the legislature entirely.
2. The common design elements: independent commission, explicit neutrality criteria (prohibit partisan data), public transparency requirements. None mandate a specific algorithm.
3. Adding an algorithmic requirement to the standard commission model is the next-generation reform: it converts "use neutral criteria" (which allows human judgment to creep in) to "use the bisect algorithm" (which removes human judgment entirely).
4. A model ballot initiative language is developed that adds algorithmic requirements to the California Prop 20 framework, serving as a template for future initiatives.
