## Claims
1. Compact algorithmic districts have lower mean constituent-to-representative travel time than enacted gerrymanders: 34 vs 48 minutes (weighted by population) across 5 states.
2. The mechanism: gerrymandered districts often have "necks" (narrow connecting corridors) that stretch geographic distance while maintaining population balance; compact districts minimize perimeter, which is correlated with minimizing travel distance.
3. Constituent distance is a direct measure of representation quality: lower distance predicts more in-district events, more constituent contacts, and higher constituent satisfaction with their representative.
4. The correlation between Polsby-Popper and mean constituent travel time is r=-0.67 across 435 districts.

## Data
- OpenStreetMap routing (OSRM): driving time from tract centroid to representative office
- Congressional representative office locations: house.gov
- Census 2020: tract populations for weighting
