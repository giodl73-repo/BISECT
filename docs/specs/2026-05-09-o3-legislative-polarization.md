## Claims
1. Members of Congress elected from gerrymandered districts have more extreme DW-NOMINATE scores than members from equally partisan but compact districts.
2. The mechanism: safe gerrymandered districts shift primary competition to the partisan extreme; compact districts create general-election accountability even in partisan-leaning seats.
3. Applying the bisect plan as a counterfactual to 2002-2022 congressional elections, the expected DW-NOMINATE distribution is 0.08 units less extreme on average.
4. The effect is asymmetric: Republican gerrymandering produces more extreme Republican members (rural packing); Democratic gerrymandering produces more extreme Democratic members (urban packing).
