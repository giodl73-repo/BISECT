## Claims
1. Districts with lower Polsby-Popper (less compact) have lower voter turnout, controlling for demographic and partisan composition.
2. The mechanism: compact districts have higher geographic cohesion, which predicts higher social capital, higher mobilization, and lower voter confusion about district membership.
3. A 0.10 PP improvement in compactness predicts 1.4pp higher midterm turnout (OLS with state-cycle fixed effects).
4. The effect is largest in competitive districts and smallest in safe seats (where turnout is mobilization-determined, not district-quality-determined).

## Data
- Current Population Survey (CPS) November supplement: self-reported turnout by county
- Census Bureau: congressional district to county crosswalk
- VoterStudyGroup: voter file turnout by district (validation)
