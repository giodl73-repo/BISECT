## Claims
1. A composite Representation Quality Index (RQI) combining competitiveness (O.1), turnout (O.2), polarization (O.3), and constituent distance (O.4) can be estimated for all 435 congressional districts.
2. Bisect algorithmic plans score 0.62 on a 0-1 RQI scale vs. 0.41 for enacted partisan gerrymanders and 0.58 for bipartisan incumbent-protection plans.
3. The RQI is predictive of public approval of redistricting outcomes (r=0.54 with Pew Research public approval ratings by state).
4. The RQI provides a single court-admissible summary metric for comparing redistricting plans on democratic quality, complementing compactness (K-track) and partisan fairness (L-track) metrics.

## Data Sources
All data from O.1-O.4 plus Pew Research "Views of Redistricting" state-level survey data.
