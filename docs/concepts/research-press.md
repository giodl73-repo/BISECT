# Research Press

## Short Version

The research press is the public curation layer for Bisect. It turns the large
workbench of papers, specs, reviews, and runs into staged journal issues.

The key rule: **seed briefs propose claims; locked issues publish reviewed
claims.**

## Journals

| Journal | Role |
|---------|------|
| District Studies | What the research shows before adoption arguments. |
| The Districting Review | How practitioners would use or adopt the algorithmic baseline. |
| The Evidence Standard | How to prove, verify, and challenge claims in legal/statistical settings. |

## Issue States

| State | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| seed | Issue brief only; candidate claims and candidate papers. |
| audition | 2-3x paper candidates are being compared for every slot. |
| provisional | Lineup selected; source-chain work begins. |
| review | Role panel and technical/legal/statistical review are active. |
| locked | Public issue is ready to show. |
| archived | Retired, corrected, superseded, or held for risk. |

## Claim Classes

Every public claim should carry a class before review:

- Descriptive: what the data, run, or map output shows.
- Empirical: statistical claim with method and uncertainty.
- Legal: statutory, constitutional, or court-facing claim.
- Operational: workflow, audit, procurement, or implementation claim.
- Advocacy: recommendation separated from evidence.
