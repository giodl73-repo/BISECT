# The Districting Review

The Districting Review is a practitioner journal for redistricting commissioners, state legislative staff, civic reform teams, and legal counsel. It presents Bisect as a deployable algorithmic baseline: what the tool can and cannot see, what it produces on public data, and what adoption language would need to specify.

The journal should be useful even to a skeptical practitioner. It should show concrete maps and workflows, but it should not turn implementation claims into legal conclusions before BOUNDARY, WARD, COVENANT, and SCALE review.

## Public Posture

- Use "input-exclusion defense" before "impossibility defense" in locked copy.
- Say what Bisect does not ingest, not what no algorithm could ever do.
- Keep model statutes and court orders labeled as drafts.
- Tie every practitioner claim to a command, manifest, or reproducible output.
