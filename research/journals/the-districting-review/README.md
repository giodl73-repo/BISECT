# The Districting Review

The Districting Review is a practitioner journal for redistricting commissioners, state legislative staff, and legal counsel. It presents the bisect algorithm as a deployable tool, showing what it produces, why it is neutral by architecture, and how to adopt it through each of the four available pathways.

Reading The Districting Review, a commissioner comes away with: concrete examples of algorithmic maps (NC 7D/7R, 223D/209R nationally), the impossibility defense against "it can be tuned," and draft statutory, initiative, court order, and commission protocol language ready to use.
