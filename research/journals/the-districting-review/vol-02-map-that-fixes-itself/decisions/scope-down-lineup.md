# Scope-Down Lineup

## Decision

Use a **scope-down lineup** for Vol. 2.

The issue should show concrete output and reproducibility, but it should not
try to prove outcomes, law, or statistical superiority in the same pass.

## Approved Working Lineup

| Order | Piece | Source posture | Claim boundary |
|-------|-------|----------------|----------------|
| 1 | Let Me Show You What It Makes | B.11 frame | No grand theory |
| 2 | The North Carolina Result | B.11 | Source-bound output |
| 3 | The National Picture | B.11/A.0 | Source-bound output |
| 4 | What Compactness Looks Like in Ensemble Space | G.1/S.1 | ESS-corrected percentile language |
| 5 | Any Party Can Verify This | A.4/R.4 | Reproducibility only |
| 6 | VRA Mode Needs Caveats | D.1/A.0 | Caveated example |
| 7 | Stability Across Census Cycles | C.2/B.15 | Scoped stability claim |
| 8 | What the Numbers Do Not Prove | review panel | Guardrail |

## Rationale

Practitioners respond to visible outputs. But the issue is strongest when it
keeps the caveats visible: a specific command, a specific version, a specific
run, a specific comparison class.

## Blocked From Preview

- Turning a source-bound output into a universal legal conclusion.
- Using raw percentile language without ESS correction.
- Treating VRA results as universally transferable.
