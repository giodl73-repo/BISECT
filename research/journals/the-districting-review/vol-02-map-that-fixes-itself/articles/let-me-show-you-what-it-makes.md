# Let Me Show You What It Makes

The first thing practitioners want is the map itself. That is reasonable. The
job of this issue is to make the output visible before anyone asks for a legal
or statistical conclusion.

The demonstration posture matters because it resets the conversation. Instead of
talking about abstract neutrality, the reader sees a concrete plan produced from
public data and a known run.

This does not answer whether the map is better in every sense. It answers the
more basic question: what does the system actually produce?
