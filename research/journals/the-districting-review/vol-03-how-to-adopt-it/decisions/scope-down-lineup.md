# Scope-Down Lineup

## Decision

Use a **scope-down lineup** for Vol. 3.

The issue should give practitioners draftable pathway language while keeping
each pathway inside its own legal and operational lane.

## Approved Working Lineup

| Order | Piece | Source posture | Claim boundary |
|-------|-------|----------------|----------------|
| 1 | The Reform Paradox | P.0 | Framing only |
| 2 | The Districting Integrity Act | P.1 | Federal model, non-commandeering limits visible |
| 3 | Ballot Initiative Language | P.2 | State-specific draft |
| 4 | Commission Integration Protocol | P.3 | Operating rule, not universal mandate |
| 5 | Court-Ordered Algorithmic Remedies | P.4 | Remedy-specific templates |
| 6 | Implementation Costs and Procurement | P.5 | Assumption-bound cost story |
| 7 | What Adoption Language Should and Should Not Say | review panel | Guardrail |

## Rationale

Adoption becomes possible when the language is concrete. The issue should help
readers draft, not just agree.

## Blocked From Preview

- Universal mandate language.
- Non-commandeering violations hidden by omission.
- Procurement language that reads like law rather than a sample RFP.
- Court-order language that forgets the remedy context.
