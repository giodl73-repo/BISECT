# The Input-Exclusion Defense

This volume explains the practitioner claim behind Bisect: the baseline workflow
does not ingest partisan data, so the tool is structurally unable to optimize
for partisan outcome in its default mode.

## Current Posture

The issue is in audition. It should be useful to commissioners, legal staff, and
practitioners without overstating the legal force of the input-exclusion
argument.

Do not draft this issue as a constitutional verdict. It is a practitioner
explanation of what the architecture does, what it excludes, and how to describe
that accurately.

## Gates

- BOUNDARY: do not convert architectural incapability into universal legal impossibility.
- WARD: keep state-law and commission-rule differences visible where they matter.
- COVENANT: every practitioner claim needs a command, manifest, or reproducible output.
- SCALE: bounded variation is a defense only if the scope is stated precisely.
