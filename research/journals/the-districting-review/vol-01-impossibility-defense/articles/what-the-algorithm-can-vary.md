# What the Algorithm Can Vary

The guardrail article should say the quiet part plainly: the algorithm can vary
within the space allowed by the inputs.

That means the defense is not "the algorithm never changes." It is "the
algorithm changes only within the scope of the documented criteria, data, and
parameters." That is a real and useful distinction.

This matters because practitioners need a defendable vocabulary. If they say
the tool is incapable of all variation, they will lose credibility. If they say
the tool excludes partisan inputs and bounds residual variation, they can make
an accurate record.
