## Read Order

Read the journals in order: District Studies → The Districting Review → The Evidence Standard.

The arc is designed to test one public proposition: whether a reproducible algorithmic baseline should be available as a federal, state, court, or commission redistricting standard. The journals do not assume that proposition is settled. They stage the case, surface objections, and promote only claims that survive review.

## Publication States

| State | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| seed | Issue brief only. Claims are candidates, not publication claims. |
| audition | Candidate papers are being reviewed for fit, strength, and non-duplication. |
| provisional | Lineup selected; articles and source-chain checks in progress. |
| review | Role panel, legal, statistical, and reproducibility review active. |
| locked | Public issue is curated, sourced, and ready to show. |
| archived | Retired, corrected, superseded, or held for risk. |

## Claim Discipline

High-stakes claims need explicit class labels before promotion:

- **Descriptive**: what the algorithm, data, or map output shows.
- **Empirical**: statistical claim requiring data, method, uncertainty, and reproducibility notes.
- **Legal**: statutory, constitutional, or court-facing claim requiring legal review.
- **Operational**: workflow, audit, implementation, or procurement claim requiring runbook verification.
- **Advocacy**: policy recommendation clearly separated from evidence.

Seed briefs may contain ambitious language. Locked issues should not. A locked issue must distinguish what is demonstrated, what is projected, what is proposed, and what remains contested.

## Rule

Once an artifact is assigned to a volume, it should not appear in an ordinary later volume. Reuse is allowed only in collected works, best-of, or corrected reprint contexts.
