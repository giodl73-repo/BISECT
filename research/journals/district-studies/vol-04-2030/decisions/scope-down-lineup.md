# Scope-Down Lineup

## Decision

Use a **scope-down lineup** for Vol. 4.

This issue should remain a planning issue. It should show the 2030 cycle as a
source of deadlines, projection work, and infrastructure requirements rather
than a settled seat-count verdict.

## Approved Working Lineup

| Order | Piece | Source posture | Claim boundary |
|-------|-------|----------------|----------------|
| 1 | Why 2030 Matters Now | Q.0 frame | No alarmist countdown language |
| 2 | Demographic Projections for 2030 | Q.1 anchor | Projection only; boundary states visible |
| 3 | Sun Belt Growth and Rust Belt Decline | Q.2 structural | Validation pending for structural recommendation |
| 4 | Data Infrastructure for 2030 | Q.3 operations | Operational, not legal, framing |
| 5 | 2030 Reapportionment Projections | Q.4 synthesis | Projection only; no settled result |
| 6 | What Must Be Ready Before 2031 | Q.0/Q.3/Q.4 guardrail | No legal or adoption conclusion |

## Rationale

The public value here is preparation. Readers should leave with a concrete
sense of what the next cycle needs: a source vintage, a validated projection
table, a resolution plan, and pre-built infrastructure. They should not leave
with the impression that the 2030 map is already decided.

## Blocked From Preview

- Treating projected seat changes as final apportionment.
- Treating the Q.2 structural recommendation as validated without a comparison run.
- Turning Q.3 procurement language into a legal mandate.
- Hiding the boundary-state uncertainty in Q.1.
