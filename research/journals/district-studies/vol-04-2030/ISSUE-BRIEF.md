## Reader Promise (expanded)

2030 redistricting begins the moment the Census releases data in April 2031. States typically have 6-12 months to complete their maps. The algorithmic redistricting infrastructure — block-group adjacency graphs, validated bisection trees, pre-registered algorithm versions — needs to be ready before that window opens. Meanwhile, demographic shifts are reshaping the map: Texas gains three seats, New York loses two, and the Sun Belt continues its decades-long absorption of congressional power. This volume makes the case that the window for establishing a federal rule is now, not later.
