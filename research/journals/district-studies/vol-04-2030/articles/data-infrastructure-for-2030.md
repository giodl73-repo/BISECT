# Data Infrastructure for 2030

The most practical 2030 lesson is that data infrastructure takes time. Block-
group adjacency graphs are larger than tract graphs, and the states that will
need them cannot build, validate, and certify those graphs at the last minute.

This article should stay operational. It should tell readers what must be
prepared, by when, and why the build matters. It should not pretend the build
itself is a legal requirement.

The planning implication is straightforward: if a state needs block-group
resolution, the graph has to exist before the redistricting window opens. That
means procurement, validation, and publication rules belong in the pre-2031
planning cycle.
