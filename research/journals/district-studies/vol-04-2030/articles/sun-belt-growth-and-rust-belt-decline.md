# Sun Belt Growth and Rust Belt Decline

The algorithmic consequence of population growth is not just that some states
gain seats. It is that the shape of the redistricting problem changes.

Fast-growing states need new district structures, often with different tree
factorizations than the previous cycle. That is a planning problem for the
partitioning pipeline, not just an electoral headline.

This article should be careful about validation. The strongest public message is
that growth patterns create structural consequences for map construction. Any
specific recommendation about ratio-optimal structure should remain labeled as
pending until comparison runs are shown.
