# Turnout And Polarization Are Not Yet Proof

Turnout and polarization are important downstream outcomes, but they are not
the strongest preview claims in Vol. 2.

The [O2] turnout source is underpowered. It can be described as directionally
suggestive, not conclusive. The [O3] polarization source is a projection. It can
be described as a hypothesis about what might change under different district
configurations, not observed legislative behavior under a new map.

That does not make the claims useless. It makes them research-program claims.
They tell the reader what the program wants to test next and what evidence would
be needed before the claim becomes public-facing.

For turnout, the preview should emphasize statistical power. For polarization,
it should emphasize identification. Those are different problems, and both need
to stay visible.
