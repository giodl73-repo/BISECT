# The RQI Question

The Representation Quality Index is useful because it tries to summarize
several democratic outcomes at once. That also makes it risky. A composite
index can clarify a pattern, but it can also hide weighting choices,
validation limits, and legal overreach.

For Vol. 2, [O5] belongs as a question rather than a conclusion. It can ask
whether competitiveness, turnout, polarization, and constituent access can be
combined into a useful public metric. It should not become a legal threshold in
District Studies.

The right public posture is curiosity with discipline. A composite index may be
helpful for comparing plans, but it should earn trust component by component.
If the component claims are uneven, the composite should be presented as an
organizing question rather than a verdict.
