# Competitiveness Is The First Political Outcome

Competitiveness is the most obvious political outcome after district lines are
drawn. A map can create safe seats, competitive seats, or something in between.
The [O1] source is therefore a strong public hook for Vol. 2.

The safe preview claim is limited: in the O.1 five-state comparison,
algorithmic plans report a higher competitive-district fraction than the
enacted comparison plans. Exact values remain gated until vote aggregation and
single-run uncertainty are documented.

This matters because competitiveness is easy for readers to understand and hard
to keep separate from reform advocacy. Vol. 2 should keep the distinction clear:
competitiveness is an outcome to measure, not an objective that the baseline
was instructed to optimize.

The preview can explain why this outcome is worth inspecting. It should not
claim that the comparison proves a general national effect.
