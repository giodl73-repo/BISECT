# What Outcome Claims Do Not Show

This issue does not show that a map mechanically causes every downstream
democratic outcome. It does not show that turnout, polarization, or public
satisfaction are settled consequences of algorithmic districting.

The issue shows something narrower: some outcome claims are ready for serious
review, some are promising but fragile, and some belong in later legal or
evidence-standard journals.

It also does not show that an algorithmic baseline should optimize these
outcomes. That distinction matters. Measuring a downstream outcome is not the
same as turning the outcome into an input, objective, or legal command.

Vol. 2 remains diagnostic. It helps the reader understand what may change after
the map and what still needs stronger evidence.
