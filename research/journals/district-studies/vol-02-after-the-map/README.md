# District Studies Vol. 2: After the Map

This issue is in **provisional public preview**. It asks which
democratic-outcome claims are strong enough to curate after Vol. 1's
geography-first diagnostic frame.

The issue is not locked publication. All causal, downstream-democracy, and
composite-index claims remain gated.

## Reader Promise

After a map is drawn, what changes for voters and representatives? This issue
auditions evidence about competitiveness, participation, polarization,
constituent access, and composite representation quality.

## Current Recommendation

Do not lead Vol. 2 with RQI. Lead with the concrete outcome pair:

1. constituent distance as the cleanest directly measured outcome; and
2. competitiveness as the policy-relevant empirical hook.

Use turnout and polarization as cautionary pieces. Use RQI as a synthesis
candidate only after its weighting and validation caveats are visible.

The approved preview path is qualitative: do not publish exact O.1 or O.4
values unless their source-chain gates close.
