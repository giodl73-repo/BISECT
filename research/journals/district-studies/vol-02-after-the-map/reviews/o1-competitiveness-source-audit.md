# O.1 Competitiveness Source Audit

## Result

O.1 is a strong audition candidate, but it should not carry exact public
headline values until source-chain and uncertainty notes are made visible.

Use O.1 as the policy hook only with careful wording: "in the O.1 comparison"
and "candidate evidence," not "confirms" or "demonstrably improves."

## What The Source Supports

The current O.1 source supports a cautious claim:

> In the O.1 five-state comparison, algorithmic plans show a higher competitive
> district fraction than the enacted plans used for comparison.

The source also supports a public explanation of why competitiveness is a
downstream outcome worth reviewing after map geometry.

## Open Source-Chain Gates

| Gate | Evidence found | Publication effect |
|------|----------------|--------------------|
| Vote reaggregation | O.1 states VEST 2020 precinct returns are areal-interpolated through `bisect label-analyze --types partisan` | Need artifact or method note before exact values |
| Single-run variance | O.1 table reports bisect vs. enacted values, but the audit found no visible seed-variance interval in the article sections reviewed | Use single-run caveat |
| State scope | Five study states: NC, WI, TX, FL, PA | Do not generalize nationally |
| Iowa benchmark | Source states bisect approaches Iowa's competitive fraction | Use as comparison, not equivalence |
| Efficiency gap sign/provenance | O.1 uses EG values; Vol. 1 keeps L.1/C.5 numeric outputs gated | Avoid EG numbers in Vol. 2 preview unless provenance closes |

## Language Allowed

- "O.1 is the strongest O-track audition candidate."
- "The five-state comparison reports more competitive districts under the
  algorithmic plans."
- "The competitiveness claim needs vote-aggregation and single-run caveats
  before exact public values are used."

## Language Blocked

- "Algorithmic plans are more competitive."
- "The outcome case is proven."
- "Bisect confirms downstream democratic improvement."
- Exact percentage-point claims without the source-chain note.

## Recommendation

Keep O.1 in the Vol. 2 lineup, but do not publish exact values until DS2-002 is
closed. It can be the first outcome article if framed as promising evidence.
