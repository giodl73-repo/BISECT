# Outcome Claim Audit

## Result

Vol. 2 can enter **audition**, but it should not enter provisional preview yet.

The O-track is promising, but claim strength is uneven. O.1 and O.4 are the
best anchors. O.2, O.3, and O.5 need visible caveats before public drafting.

## Board Review

| Role | Finding | Effect |
|------|---------|--------|
| COMMONS | The reader hook is strong: "what changes after the map?" | pass |
| SCALE | O.2 is underpowered and O.3 is projected; neither should be headline proof. | blocker for strong claims |
| DATUM | O.1 and O.4 need source-chain notes before numeric public copy. | blocker for exact values |
| BOUNDARY | O.5 legal-testimony language belongs to The Evidence Standard, not District Studies. | block legal framing |
| WARD | Do not convert RQI into a candidate legal threshold in this issue. | block legal threshold |
| COVENANT | Routing, address, vote aggregation, and replication artifacts need review. | source-chain gate |

## Safe Public Framing

> Vol. 2 auditions downstream outcome claims. Competitiveness and constituent
> distance appear to be the strongest candidates; turnout, polarization, and RQI
> require more caveating before public claims.

## Do Not Say

- "The map causes higher turnout."
- "Algorithmic plans reduce polarization."
- "RQI is court-admissible."
- "A plan below 0.50 RQI fails legally."
- "The outcome case is proven."
