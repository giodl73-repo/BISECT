# Scope-Down Lineup

## Decision

Use a **scope-down lineup** for Vol. 2.

The issue should not attempt to prove the full democratic-outcomes case. It
should show how downstream outcome claims are auditioned, with the cleanest
claims first and the fragile claims visibly caveated.

## Approved Working Lineup

| Order | Piece | Source posture | Claim boundary |
|-------|-------|----------------|----------------|
| 1 | Editorial: After the Map | O.0 frame | No proof claim |
| 2 | Distance Is A Real Cost | O.4 anchor | Counterfactual and routing assumptions visible |
| 3 | Competitiveness Is The First Political Outcome | O.1 anchor | Five-state comparison; exact values gated |
| 4 | Turnout And Polarization Are Not Yet Proof | O.2/O.3 caution | Suggestive/projection only |
| 5 | The RQI Question | O.5 synthesis | No legal threshold or court-admissible language |
| 6 | What Outcome Claims Do Not Show | O review panel | Required guardrail |

## Rationale

O.4 is the cleanest measured outcome because travel distance is directly
computable from geometry, routing, and office-location assumptions. O.1 is the
strongest policy hook but needs vote-aggregation and single-run caveats before
exact values are promoted. O.2, O.3, and O.5 are useful because they show the
ambition of the outcomes program, but they should not carry headline proof in
this issue.

## Blocked From Preview

- Exact O.1 percentage-point headline values.
- Exact O.4 travel-time savings.
- O.2 turnout causal claims.
- O.3 polarization causal claims.
- O.5 legal-testimony language.
- RQI threshold language.
