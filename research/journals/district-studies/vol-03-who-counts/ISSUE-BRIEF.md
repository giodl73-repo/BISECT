## Reader Promise (expanded)

"One person, one vote" assumes we know which population base a redistricting plan should use. The answer is contested across at least four dimensions: total population, voting-age population, citizen voting-age population, and adjusted counts that address where incarcerated people are physically located versus their home communities. This volume takes each dimension seriously and tests whether total population with prison adjustment can be stated as a public recommendation after source and legal review.

## Note on N.2 and N.4

College students and military overseas are real counting issues but are not feature anchors for this volume. N.2 needs derivation support for its national campus-counting estimate. N.4 describes a planned civilian-only mode that should be labeled as Phase 2 unless implementation evidence exists.
