## Reader Promise (expanded)

"One person, one vote" assumes we know who counts as a person for redistricting purposes. We don't — or rather, the answer is contested across at least four dimensions: total population, voting-age population, citizen voting-age population, and adjusted counts that correct for where incarcerated people are physically located versus where they politically belong. This volume takes each dimension seriously and arrives at a recommendation: total population with prison adjustment only.

## Note on N.2 and N.4

College students and military overseas are real counting issues but smaller in magnitude than prison gerrymandering. They are archived notes in this volume, not feature articles. The 180-tract college estimate (N.2) and the 600K military overseas (N.4) deserve acknowledgment but don't drive the argument.
