# Population Claim Audit

## Board Findings

| Lens | Finding | Disposition |
|------|---------|-------------|
| BOUNDARY | Population-base language crosses quickly into constitutional law. Evenwel allows total population and leaves other questions open; the issue must not imply a universal legal answer. | gate |

| CONTOUR | Prison-adjusted, CVAP, PL 94-171, ACS, and group-quarters data need separate provenance notes. | gate |

| COMMONS | Prison gerrymandering is the right public entry point because it makes the abstract input question concrete. | pass |

## Source Dispositions

### N.1 Prison Gerrymandering

Use as the lead hook. Strip or bracket claims that sound like legal findings, especially vote-dilution and Gingles-adjacent language. Make clear when a `prison_adjusted` balance mode is a planned preprocessing extension rather than a current implementation.

### N.3 Citizen VAP

Use as the hard case, not as a partisan-direction headline. The public version can say the population base would change representation incentives and district construction, but exact national seat-shift language is blocked until uncertainty bounds exist.

### N.5 Population Definition Comparison

Use as the synthesis candidate. Do not call it definitive or a permanent litigation reference in public copy until the 50-state comparison and prison-adjusted coverage are complete or formally scoped down.

### N.2 and N.4 Archive Notes

Keep as archive notes. N.2 needs derivation support for its national estimate. N.4 needs Phase 2 labeling unless civilian-only implementation evidence is produced.

## Recommended Path

Proceed to a qualitative preview path if the issue can avoid exact national estimates and legal recommendations. Otherwise hold at audition until N.3 uncertainty, N.5 coverage, and N.1 implementation evidence close.
