# Qualitative Preview Path

## Decision

Use the **qualitative preview path** for District Studies Vol. 3.

Vol. 3 can become useful public work before the exact N.3 and N.5 artifacts are
closed, but only if it avoids exact numeric claims and legal conclusions. The
preview should explain the structure of the population-base problem, not claim
to solve it.

## What This Allows

- Use [N1] to show why prison residence is the cleanest public example of the counting problem.
- Use [N0] to explain the population-base menu.
- Use [N3] to explain why citizen VAP is legally and empirically sensitive.
- Use [N5] to frame the comparison question.
- Use [N2] and [N4] as archive notes that show the problem is broader than prisons and CVAP.

## What This Blocks

- Exact national seat-shift estimates.
- Legal advice or universal adoption language.
- Vote-dilution conclusions.
- Definitive-table or permanent-litigation-reference language.
- Implementation claims for planned balance modes.

## Rationale

The public value of Vol. 3 is definitional clarity. A reader should leave
understanding that population equality is not self-executing: the population
vector is a policy and data choice. That lesson does not require exact values.
