# Scope-Down Lineup

## Decision

Use a **scope-down lineup** for Vol. 3.

The issue should not try to settle the legal population-base question. It should
show that population equality depends on a definitional input, then audition the
strongest candidate inputs without treating any one of them as final law.

## Approved Working Lineup

| Order | Piece | Source posture | Claim boundary |
|-------|-------|----------------|----------------|
| 1 | Editorial: Counting Is a Choice | N.0 frame | No legal recommendation |

| 5 | The 50-State Comparison Question | N.5 synthesis | No "definitive" or litigation-reference framing |
| 6 | What This Issue Does Not Settle | N.2/N.4/review panel | Archive and guardrail piece |

## Rationale

Prison gerrymandering is the most accessible example because it makes the input
question concrete: the same algorithm can produce different maps depending on
where incarcerated people are counted. Citizen VAP is essential because it is
the hardest legal and empirical contrast, but it should be presented as a
boundary case. N.5 should organize the comparison, not carry a final statutory
recommendation.

## Blocked From Preview

- Exact national seat-shift language from N.3.
- Any claim that citizen VAP is required or forbidden across all contexts.
- Any claim that total population plus prison adjustment is the final statutory answer.
- Any claim that the N.5 table is definitive while coverage gaps remain.
- N.2 national tract estimates without derivation.
- N.4 civilian-only implementation language without implementation evidence.
