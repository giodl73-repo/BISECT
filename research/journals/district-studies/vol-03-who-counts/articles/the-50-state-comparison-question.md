# The 50-State Comparison Question

A good population-base rule should survive comparison. It should not be defended
only by one vivid example or one favorable state.

That is why the 50-state comparison is the natural synthesis for this issue. A
complete comparison would ask the same questions across population definitions:
how much territory changes, whether compactness changes, which communities are
most affected, how data uncertainty enters, and whether the effect is
concentrated or broad.

That comparison is also where caution matters most. A table can look final even
when some columns still depend on unresolved coverage, provenance, or estimation
questions. For this preview, the comparison should be framed as the question the
issue is building toward, not as a closed litigation reference.

The likely public lesson is still useful: population definition is not a
technical footnote. It is one of the inputs that determines what the map is
allowed to balance.
