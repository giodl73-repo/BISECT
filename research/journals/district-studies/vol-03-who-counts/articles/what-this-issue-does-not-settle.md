# What This Issue Does Not Settle

This issue makes the population-base question visible. It does not settle every
answer.

It does not choose a final statutory population definition. Total population
with prison adjustment is a candidate option for review, not a locked public
recommendation in this volume.

It does not decide every legal context. Congressional districts, state
legislative districts, local districts, and state constitutional rules can raise
different questions. A public article cannot collapse them into one universal
answer.

It does not publish exact national shift estimates for citizen VAP. Those
numbers require uncertainty bounds and jurisdictional review before they can
carry public weight.

It does not treat every edge case as equal. Student counting and military or
overseas counting are real issues, but in this issue they remain archive notes
unless their derivation and implementation gates close.

It does not prove that the algorithm has solved the population problem. A map
drawn from clean rules can still inherit a disputed input definition. That is
the central lesson: input integrity is part of redistricting integrity.
