# G.1 SCALE/DATUM Review

## Result

The draft note `drafts/g1-ensemble-median-case.md` is approved for audition
language. It is not approved for exact percentile claims in locked public copy.

The safe public phrase is:

> In the G.1 ensemble run, the Bisect plan lands near the middle of the
> compactness distribution for North Carolina. The estimate is preliminary until
> multi-chain validation confirms the ensemble position.

## Evidence

The current G.1 source supports a cautious statement:

- ReCom ensembles use 1,000 steps.
- The source says 1,000 steps provide a preliminary distributional portrait.
- The source says multi-seed Phase 2 validation is required to confirm mixing.
- The source estimates NC-scale effective sample size near 70.
- The source states that all G.1 percentile figures are preliminary point
  estimates.
- `data/nc_ensemble.json` records a percentile value of `49.8`.

## SCALE Finding

The draft's uncertainty phrasing is strong enough for audition because it avoids
the exact 49.8/50.0 percentile claim and does not treat 1,000 chain steps as
independent samples.

Blocked for locked copy:

- exact percentile language;
- "certified ensemble position";
- legal certificate language;
- claims that geography determines the result.

Allowed for locked copy after light review:

- "near the middle";
- "in this ensemble run";
- "preliminary point estimate";
- "effective sample size and multi-chain validation remain caveats."

## DATUM Finding

The draft is traceable to current G.1 source and data. It should remain a
summary of current evidence, not a restatement of stronger source sections that
use legal-certificate or inevitability language.

## BOUNDARY Finding

Do not import the G.1 legal-argument section into Vol. 1. Phrases such as
"certificate" or "geography determines the outcome" are not suitable for
District Studies Vol. 1 public copy.

## Promotion Rule

DS1-004 may be marked reviewed for audition. Before locked publication, the
article summary should receive a final pass verifying that no exact percentile
or legal-certificate language entered during rewriting.
