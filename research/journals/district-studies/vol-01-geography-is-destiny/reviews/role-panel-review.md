# Role Panel Review

## Summary

The issue is ready for audition, not publication. The central frame is strong if
kept descriptive: geography strongly constrains partisan outcomes. The risk is
turning that descriptive claim into a legal, causal, or inevitability claim.

## Role Notes

| Role | Review note | Gate |
|------|-------------|------|
| MERIDIAN | T.5 and G.1 need exact algorithm/run parameters before claim lock. | Confirm source/run reproducibility. |
| BOUNDARY | Geography findings cannot be treated as proof of constitutional validity or invalidity. | Remove legal conclusions from articles. |
| CONTOUR | Any state case study needs data vintage, geography level, and run manifest. | Verify source chain for selected state cases. |
| PRECINCT | Distinguish geographic sorting, intentional gerrymandering, and algorithm output. | Use plain taxonomy in editorial. |
| SCALE | G.1 percentiles and L.1 efficiency gap are not safe without caveats. | ESS and sign-convention gates. |
| DATUM | The issue needs a correction note if it uses known-risk sources. | Maintain review log. |
| COMMONS | Public title works, but body copy must say "constrains" not "determines" by default. | Accessibility pass after draft. |

## Required Before Lock

- Source-chain audit moves from `audit-seed` to pass/partial-pass/fail.
- Role review is rerun on actual article drafts.
- Any held source is either fixed, disclosed, or removed.
