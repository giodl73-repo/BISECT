## Reader Promise (expanded)

The conventional narrative says gerrymandering is about politicians drawing sneaky shapes. The research corpus asks how much of the observed partisan outcome follows from the physical distribution of voters across space. Democrats often pack into cities; Republicans are more spread across suburbs and rural areas. This volume tests the claim that compact geographic districts can produce partisan asymmetry without intentional manipulation.
