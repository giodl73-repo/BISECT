## Reader Promise (expanded)

The conventional narrative says gerrymandering is about politicians drawing sneaky shapes. The research says otherwise: partisan outcomes follow the physical distribution of voters across space. Democrats pack into cities; Republicans spread across suburbs and rural areas. Any map that draws compact geographic districts will produce a slight Republican seat advantage — not because of manipulation but because of population geography. This volume establishes that bedrock fact.
