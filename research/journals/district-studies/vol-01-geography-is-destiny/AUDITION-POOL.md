# Audition Pool

## Issue Thesis

Partisan outcomes in American redistricting are strongly constrained by voter
geography. This issue should test that thesis before making any adoption,
legal, or algorithm-advocacy claim.

## Selection Rules

- Select for explanatory power, source-chain strength, and public readability.
- Do not reuse selected papers in ordinary later volumes unless marked as a reprint.
- Keep legal adoption claims out of this issue.
- Treat ensemble percentiles, efficiency-gap signs, and causality language as review gates.

## Review Questions

- BOUNDARY: Does the issue imply legal intent or unconstitutional conduct when it only shows geography?
- PRECINCT: Does the issue distinguish geographic sorting from partisan manipulation?
- SCALE: Are ensemble percentiles, efficiency-gap signs, and uncertainty stated correctly?
- DATUM: Are claims traceable to papers, reviews, data, and run context?
- COMMONS: Can a public reader understand the diagnosis without metric fluency?
