# District Studies

District Studies is a research digest for political scientists, policy researchers, and engaged citizens. It presents the bisect redistricting research program as a series of empirical arguments, building from the foundational claim (geography determines partisan outcomes) through the outcomes case (algorithmic redistricting improves democracy) to the urgency argument (2030 is coming).

Reading District Studies, a skeptic comes away convinced that: (1) gerrymandering is a real and measurable problem; (2) algorithmic redistricting produces measurably better outcomes; (3) the inputs to the algorithm are contested and must be specified by statute; and (4) the clock is running.
