# The Evidence Standard

The Evidence Standard is a legal and statistical journal for litigators, expert witnesses, federal legislators, and courts. It presents the statistical and cryptographic basis for algorithmic redistricting evidence, culminating in the answer to the strongest objection: "Can it be gamed?"

Reading The Evidence Standard, a litigator comes away with: calibrated p-values rather than raw percentiles, a 43-second verification protocol for plan authenticity, and a formal proof that the maximum gaming effect under DIA pre-registration constraints is less than half a congressional seat nationally.

The Evidence Standard is the final journal in the arc. A reader who has completed District Studies and The Districting Review arrives here ready to vote for the federal rule. This journal gives them the statistical and adversarial arguments they need to defend that vote.
