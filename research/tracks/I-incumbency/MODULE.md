# I — Incumbency

**Theme**: How algorithmically-drawn congressional maps affect incumbents — pairing
probability, safe-seat creation/destruction, open-seat generation, and the legal
status of incumbency protection as a redistricting criterion.

The algorithm takes no incumbency input; outcomes are measured ex post against
the 2022 incumbent address database and VEST election data.

## Track Chain

I.0 (overview) → I.1 (pairing probability) → I.2 (safe-seat creation/destruction)
              → I.3 (open-seat effects) → I.4 (legal criterion analysis)

## Data Requirements

- 2022 incumbent home addresses (public record, FEC filings)
- VEST 2020 presidential precinct returns mapped to 2020 census tracts
- Bisect plan outputs for NC ($k=14$), WI ($k=8$), TX ($k=38$)

## Contracts

I.1: Report probability that any two incumbents from same party are paired
in same district under bisect vs. enacted map.
I.2: Report fraction of districts with partisan lean > 15 pp (safe seats)
under bisect vs. enacted.
I.3: Report expected number of open seats (no incumbent) generated by bisect
redistricting vs. enacted post-2020 maps.
I.4: Cite actual case law on incumbency as a permissible redistricting factor.
