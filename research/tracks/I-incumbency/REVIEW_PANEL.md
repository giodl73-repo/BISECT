## Track-Level Strengths
- The incumbency-neutral baseline (I.0/I.1) is a novel, operationalizable contribution.
- The enacted-map comparisons throughout (I.1–I.3) provide vivid evidence of deliberate incumbency protection that algorithmic maps cannot replicate.
- I.4 is the track's strongest paper — the legal analysis is comprehensive and correctly captures the Karcher/Cox/LULAC trifecta.

## Cross-Paper Issues
1. **Single-seed limitation**: All five papers flag single-seed results as preliminary. This is consistent and honest, but the track would benefit from a shared Phase 2 plan specifying which seeds will be run for multi-seed validation.
2. **Baseline specification**: I.0/I.1 use an analytical baseline ($p = 1/(k-1)$); the Duchin/Rodden reviewers want ensemble-based validation. This should be addressed consistently across the track.
3. **Rucho post-landscape**: I.4 mentions Rucho but I.0–I.3 do not address how the foreclosure of federal partisan gerrymandering review affects incumbency-based claims. One cross-track paragraph in I.0 would cover this.
