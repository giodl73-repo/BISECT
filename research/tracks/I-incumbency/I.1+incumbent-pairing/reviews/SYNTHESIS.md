# Review Synthesis — I.1: Incumbent-Pairing Probability
**Round**: 1 | **Score**: 2.8/4 | **Verdict**: Conditional Accept

## Panel Scores
Jacobson 3/4, Pildes 3/4, Rodden 2/4, Stephanopoulos 3/4, Duchin 2/4

## Consensus Strengths
The analytical derivation of the pairing baseline $p_{\text{pair}} = 1/(k-1)$ for exchangeable tracts is clean and interpretable. The enacted-map comparison (zero pairings in NC and WI) is the paper's most powerful result. The three-state coverage gives breadth.

## P1 Items
- **P1-A** (Duchin, Rodden): The independence assumption underlying $p_{\text{pair}} = 1/(k-1)$ requires validation. Adjacent districts share boundaries; pairing events are not independent. Validate via ReCom ensemble (G.1 NC data available) or provide formal argument for approximate independence.
- **P1-B** (Duchin): Binomial test for enacted-vs-baseline comparison requires exchangeability assumption; use permutation test instead, or clearly label the test as approximate.
- **P1-C** (Jacobson): Single-seed limitation must be in abstract, not footnote.

## P2 Items
- Add LULAC v. Perry citation (Pildes).
- Clarify pairing rate denominator — per-incumbent vs. per-pair (Rodden).
- Multi-seed validation for at least NC (Duchin) — note as Phase 2 extension.
