# Review — I.0: Incumbency and Algorithmic Redistricting: Overview
**Reviewer**: Nicholas Stephanopoulos (Election law, quantitative legal analysis)
**Round**: 1
**Score**: 3/4
**Verdict**: Minor Revision

## Summary
The paper correctly identifies that incumbency-blind redistricting is legally preferable for neutrality — it cannot be accused of targeting incumbents of either party. The quantitative comparison to enacted maps is the most useful contribution for legal practitioners: the zero-pairing rates in enacted NC and WI maps are vivid evidence of deliberate incumbency protection.

## Concerns
- **Expert witness applicability**: The paper should explicitly recommend how this framework would be deployed in redistricting litigation. What testimony would an expert based on this paper give? What would opposing experts say?
- **Open-seat effects and community representation**: The paper focuses on incumbent pairing and safe-seat dynamics but does not address how open-seat creation under algorithmic redistricting affects community representation continuity — an issue distinct from incumbency protection per se.

## Required Changes
- **P2**: Add a subsection on expert witness deployment — how this framework supports algorithmic redistricting in litigation.
- **P2**: Note open-seat effects on community representation continuity (cross-reference I.3).
