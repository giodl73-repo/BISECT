# Review — I.0: Incumbency and Algorithmic Redistricting: Overview
**Reviewer**: Gary Jacobson (Congressional elections, incumbent advantage)
**Round**: 1
**Score**: 3/4
**Verdict**: Minor Revision

## Summary
I.0 provides a useful synthesis of how incumbency-blind redistricting interacts with incumbent welfare. The incumbency-neutral baseline concept is the paper's most valuable contribution: it provides a principled counterfactual against which to evaluate both algorithmic and enacted maps. The finding that enacted maps significantly deviate from the baseline (deliberate protection) while algorithmic maps stay within range is compelling. The single-seed limitation is the primary weakness.

## Strengths
- The incumbency-neutral baseline is a genuinely novel contribution. Prior work on redistricting and incumbency has not clearly defined what "neutral" means; this paper provides a defensible operationalization.
- The three-mechanism taxonomy (geographic restructuring, partisan composition shift, constituent base reassignment) is well-organized and covers the major pathways.
- The comparison to enacted maps — where zero NC and WI incumbent pairings demonstrate deliberate protection — is striking and well-presented.

## Concerns
- **Single-seed results**: All findings are marked preliminary (single seed $s=42$). For a synthesis paper that makes claims across I.1–I.4, the multi-seed qualification needs to be more prominent. The abstract should lead with the limitation, not bury it in footnotes.
- **NC open-seat count 4 exceeding [0,3] CI**: This is flagged as a "pairing effect" exceeding the baseline, but the statistical treatment is informal. If this is the paper's one finding that exceeds the neutral range, it deserves more careful analysis — is it systematically reproducible or a seed-specific artifact?
- **Legal asymmetry section**: The paper notes that incumbency protection is not prohibited but incumbency harm is also not a cognisable claim. This asymmetry deserves a paragraph citing relevant case law (Baker v. Carr, Thornburg) rather than a bare assertion.

## Required Changes
- **P1**: Move single-seed limitation to abstract's first paragraph, not a footnote. Readers need to know the results are preliminary before interpreting findings.
- **P1**: Provide a formal test (or at minimum a confidence interval) for the NC 4 open seats vs. [0,3] CI exceedance. Is this boundary case stable across seeds?
- **P2**: Add legal citations to the incumbency asymmetry discussion.
