\section{Verification Equations}

RCOUNT currently exposes two manifest-layer equations:

\[
\texttt{accepted\_ballots(batch)}
  = \texttt{counted\_ballots(batch)} + \texttt{rejected\_ballots(batch)}
\]

\[
\texttt{batch\_summary\_total(batch)}
  = \texttt{counted\_ballots(batch)}
\]

The second equation is only evaluated for summary rows with a
\texttt{batch\_id}. The verifier first checks that the batch id exists in
\path{normalized/batches.ndjson}. If a summary references a missing batch, the
package fails before the batch total can be trusted.

\begin{center}
\small
\begin{tabular}{ccc}
\fbox{\begin{tabular}{c}batch manifest\\accepted, counted, rejected\end{tabular}} &
$\longleftrightarrow$ &
\fbox{\begin{tabular}{c}batch summaries\\contest counts and residuals\end{tabular}} \\
 & $\longrightarrow$ & \\
\multicolumn{3}{c}{\fbox{\begin{tabular}{c}jurisdiction summary\\public contest totals\end{tabular}}} \\
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

Jurisdiction totals roll up only after these local controls hold. A verifier
that silently treats a missing batch as zero, optional, or inferred would hide
the exact error the manifest layer is designed to catch.
