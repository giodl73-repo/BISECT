\section{Manifest Model}

The current RCOUNT batch manifest row is intentionally small:

\begin{center}
\small
\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{lX}
\toprule
Field & Meaning \\
\midrule
\texttt{batch\_id} & Stable package identifier for the batch. \\
\texttt{reporting\_unit\_id} & Public unit associated with the batch, such as a precinct or central-count unit. \\
\texttt{kind} & Operational class, such as election-day, mail, provisional, vote-center, or central-count. \\
\texttt{status} & Publication status represented in the package. \\
\texttt{accepted\_ballots} & Ballots accepted into the batch's accounting path. \\
\texttt{counted\_ballots} & Ballots counted from that batch for the represented contest summaries. \\
\texttt{rejected\_ballots} & Accepted-accounting records later rejected or removed from counting. \\
\texttt{source\_refs} & Source evidence rows that support the manifest row. \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}
\end{center}

A batch row is not necessarily a precinct, voter, ballot card, CVR row, or
contest row. It is an accounting control. The later CVR paper can reconcile
contest selections inside counted ballots; V.5 only checks whether the batch
containers named by summaries exist and balance.
