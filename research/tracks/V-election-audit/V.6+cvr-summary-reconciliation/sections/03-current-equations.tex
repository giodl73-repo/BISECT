\section{Current Equations}

The current implementation exercises both the normalized CVR aggregate and the
summary-side guardrail. The CVR equation is:

\[
\sum \texttt{normalized CVR contest rows}
  = \texttt{summary selections and residuals}.
\]

For each contest, reporting unit, status, and optional batch, the verifier
aggregates \texttt{selection\_ids}, \texttt{undervote}, \texttt{overvote}, and
\texttt{blank\_contest} fields from \path{normalized/cvr.ndjson}. It then
compares those aggregates to \path{normalized/summaries.ndjson}. A mismatch
fails as \texttt{cvr\_summary\_total}.

The summary guardrail remains \texttt{contest\_selection\_sum}:

\[
\texttt{counted\_ballots}
  = \sum \texttt{selection votes}
  + \texttt{undervotes}
  + \texttt{overvotes}
  + \texttt{blank\_contests}.
\]

RCOUNT also checks \texttt{jurisdiction\_contest\_total}, which rolls lower-level
reporting-unit summaries up to the jurisdiction summary. Together, these checks
catch arithmetic drift before a CVR adapter is allowed to claim that
lower-level rows support the public totals.
