\section{Introduction}

Cast-vote records are the interpreted contest marks exported by a voting system.
They can make public verification much stronger: instead of trusting only a
summary row, an auditor can recompute contest totals from lower-level records.
They can also make verification more dangerous if ballot-level detail exposes
rare vote patterns, ballot styles, or small-cell combinations.

V.6 defines the reconciliation contract between CVR evidence and public
summaries. The current RCOUNT crates now normalize a small
\path{normalized/cvr.ndjson} surface: one row per CVR contest interpretation.
Those rows aggregate to public summaries through \texttt{cvr\_summary\_total},
while \texttt{contest\_selection\_sum} remains the summary-side guardrail that
checks selections, write-in buckets, undervotes, overvotes, and blank contests
against declared counted ballots.
