\begin{abstract}
Risk-limiting audits depend on two separable claims: the sample was selected
from the declared ballot population by a public random process, and the sampled
ballots provide enough evidence to stop or escalate under a named statistical
method. This paper lands the first RCOUNT contract. A verifier can recompute
the contest audit population from normalized CVR rows, recompute the contest
manifest hash, replay a SHA-256 sampler from the public seed, risk limit, and
algorithm id, and compare every published sample draw. The current fixture
pair proves the replay surface: \texttt{rla-replay} passes, while
\texttt{bad-rla-replay} fails when one sampled CVR id is edited.
\end{abstract}
