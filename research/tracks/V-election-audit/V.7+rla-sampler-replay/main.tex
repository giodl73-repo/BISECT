\begin{abstract}
Risk-limiting audits depend on two separable claims: the sample was selected
from the declared ballot population by a public random process, and the sampled
ballots provide enough evidence to stop or escalate under a named rule. This
paper lands both an RCOUNT sampler-replay contract and a first stopping-check
contract. A verifier recomputes the contest audit population from normalized
CVR rows, replays a SHA-256 sampler from public seed material, checks sampled
observations against CVR interpretations, and verifies that the declared
pass-or-escalate status follows from the discrepancy threshold. The fixture
pairs prove both surfaces: \texttt{rla-replay} and \texttt{rla-stopping} pass,
while edited sample draws or observed marks fail.
\end{abstract}
