\section{Introduction}

Risk-limiting audits (RLAs) are often described as statistical audits, but a
large part of public trust sits one layer below the statistical test. Observers
must be able to ask whether the sampled ballots were actually the ballots that
the public seed and manifest should have selected.

RCOUNT therefore treats sampler replay as its own contract. The verifier does
not need to trust a vendor user interface, an exported spreadsheet, or a
hand-copied sample list. It recomputes the population and the draws from
published package data.

This paper covers the first V.7 substrate slice. It does not claim that an RLA
has stopped correctly. It claims that the sample list used by a later stopping
rule is reproducible from public evidence.
