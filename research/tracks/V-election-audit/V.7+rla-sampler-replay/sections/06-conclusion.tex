\section{Conclusion}

RCOUNT now has an implemented replay contract for the first half of an RLA:
the sample list. It also has a first observation and stopping-status contract.
The verifier recomputes the contest population hash and every draw from public
seed material, then checks that observed marks support the declared
pass-or-escalate status.

The remaining V.7 work is to replace the simple threshold rule with legally
specified statistical stopping methods. With sampler replay and observations in
place, that layer can focus on discrepancies, margins, and escalation without
also carrying the burden of proving how the sample was chosen.
