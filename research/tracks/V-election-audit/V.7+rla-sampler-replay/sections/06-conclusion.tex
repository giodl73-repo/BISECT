\section{Conclusion}

RCOUNT now has an implemented replay contract for the first half of an RLA:
the sample list. It also has a first observation and stopping-status contract.
The verifier recomputes the contest population hash and every draw from public
seed material, then checks that observed marks support the declared
pass-or-escalate status. When discrepancies are present, the verifier also
checks that the declared discrepancy taxonomy matches the computed mismatch.
Reported margin metadata is no longer free text; it is recomputed from the
public jurisdiction summary. The first named statistical method also recomputes
a declared risk estimate before accepting a stopping claim.

The remaining V.7 work is to add legally specified statistical stopping
methods, especially Kaplan-Markov or state-specific adapters, on top of the
same audit surface. With sampler replay and observations in place, that layer
can focus on jurisdictional risk calculations and escalation without also
carrying the burden of proving how the sample was chosen, what type of mismatch
occurred, or which public totals define the margin.
