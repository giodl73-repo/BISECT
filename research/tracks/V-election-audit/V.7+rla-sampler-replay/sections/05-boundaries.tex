\section{Boundaries}

This slice verifies sample selection, not RLA stopping. It does not compute a
Kaplan-Markov, ballot-polling, or ballot-level comparison risk measure. It also
does not decide whether a jurisdiction's legal RLA method permits replacement
sampling, modulo reduction, or a particular manifest construction.

Those boundaries are deliberate. V.7 now has a replayable sampler surface that
later work can reuse. The next layer should add observation records,
discrepancy types, margin metadata, stopping rules, escalation rules, and
state-specific sampler adapters.

The privacy boundary also matters. A public RLA sample can reveal ballot-level
records selected for audit. RCOUNT can make the sample reproducible, but
jurisdictions still need publication rules for small batches, rare ballot
styles, and any join between CVRs and voter-facing inclusion tokens.
