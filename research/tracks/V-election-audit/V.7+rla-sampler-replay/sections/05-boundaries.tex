\section{Boundaries}

This slice verifies sample selection and a deliberately small stopping
contract. It does not compute a Kaplan-Markov, ballot-polling, or full
ballot-level comparison risk measure. It also does not decide whether a
jurisdiction's legal RLA method permits replacement sampling, modulo
reduction, or a particular manifest construction.

Those boundaries are deliberate. V.7 now has a replayable sampler surface, a
minimal observation-to-status surface, and explicit discrepancy taxonomy that
later work can reuse. The next layer should add margin metadata, Kaplan-Markov
or jurisdiction-specific stopping math, escalation workflows, and state-specific
sampler adapters.

The privacy boundary also matters. A public RLA sample can reveal ballot-level
records selected for audit. RCOUNT can make the sample reproducible, but
jurisdictions still need publication rules for small batches, rare ballot
styles, and any join between CVRs and voter-facing inclusion tokens.
