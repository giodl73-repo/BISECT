\section{Sampler Contract}

The implemented audit record stores:

\begin{center}
\small
\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{lX}
\toprule
Field & Meaning \\
\midrule
\texttt{audit\_id} & Stable id for the audit round. \\
\texttt{contest\_id} & Contest whose CVR rows define the audit population. \\
\texttt{risk\_limit\_ppm} & Risk limit in parts per million, avoiding floating-point drift. \\
\texttt{public\_seed} & Public random seed, such as a dice-generated seed ceremony value. \\
\texttt{sampling\_algorithm\_id} & Versioned sampler name. Current value: \texttt{rcount-sha256-modulo-v1}. \\
\texttt{manifest\_hash} & Hash of the sorted unique CVR ids for the contest. \\
\texttt{sample\_size} & Number of draws in this replay round. \\
\texttt{sample\_draws} & Published draw index and selected CVR id pairs. \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}
\end{center}

The population is the sorted unique set of \texttt{cvr\_id} values for the
target contest. Draw $i$ hashes the domain prefix, manifest hash, public seed,
contest id, risk limit, draw index, and algorithm id. The first eight digest
bytes are interpreted as a little-endian integer and reduced modulo the
population length.

This modulo sampler is intentionally simple and versioned. A future statutory
or state-specific sampler can live beside it under a different algorithm id.
