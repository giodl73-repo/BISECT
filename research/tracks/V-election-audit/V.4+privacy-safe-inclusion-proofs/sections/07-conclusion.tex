\section{Conclusion}

Privacy-safe inclusion is a narrow but valuable promise. RCOUNT can let a public
package carry anonymized accepted or counted token evidence, but the package
must not carry candidate choices, direct voter identity, or linkable small-cell
facts. The positive and negative fixtures establish the baseline: an anonymized
token can pass, while a choice-bearing proof must fail.

That line protects the rest of the V track. Public verification should make
counts more accountable without making voters more coercible. The next papers
can add ballot manifest and CVR reconciliation evidence on the accounting side,
but V.4 fixes the public privacy invariant they must preserve.
