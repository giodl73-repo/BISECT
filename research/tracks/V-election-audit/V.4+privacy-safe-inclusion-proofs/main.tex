\begin{abstract}
Voters may reasonably want assurance that an accepted or counted record exists
without receiving a receipt that proves how they voted. This paper defines the
baseline RCOUNT privacy gate for inclusion-style proofs. A public proof may
carry an anonymized token hash and a reporting-unit reference, but it must not
carry candidate selections, direct voter identity, ballot style, timestamps, or
other linkable facts that create coercion or vote-buying risk. Using the
\texttt{privacy-inclusion-sketch} and \texttt{choice-bearing-proof} fixtures, we
show how RCOUNT can reject choice-bearing artifacts while preserving a narrow
path toward receipt-safe public verification.
\end{abstract}
