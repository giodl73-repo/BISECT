## Summary

The paper is well framed around the correct boundary: RCOUNT verifies public
evidence and arithmetic, while election officials certify under law. The
introduction and boundaries section are unusually careful for a first draft.
The synthetic fixture ladder also helps avoid fraud-centric framing: corrected
canvass and late mail are described as legitimate state changes that need
lineage.

## Major Issues

1. **Certification states need a clearer lifecycle table.** The paper names
   unofficial, canvassed, recounted, amended, and certified, but does not show
   which RCOUNT records correspond to each state or what events can move a
   package between states.

2. **Ballot acceptance and voter eligibility are under-separated.** The paper
   says RCOUNT does not decide voter eligibility, but V.0 should state where
   accepted/rejected/cured/provisional ballot decisions appear as evidence,
   especially because later V papers will discuss canvass arithmetic.

3. **Jurisdiction variation is acknowledged but thin.** The draft should say
   explicitly that RCOUNT v0 fixtures are synthetic and jurisdiction-neutral,
   not a claim that one national canvass workflow exists.

## Minor Issues

- Define "canvassed" the first time it appears.
- The abstract mentions certification but not canvassing boards or statutory
  events.
- The conclusion should repeat that a passing transcript is not an official
  result.

## Strengths

- Correctly avoids equating corrected totals with tampering.
- Good role framing around CANVASS/TALLY/VAULT.
- Strong non-goals section.
