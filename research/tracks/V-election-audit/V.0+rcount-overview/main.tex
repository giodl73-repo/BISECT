\begin{abstract}
Election results become public facts through a mixture of voting-system exports,
batch accounting, canvass decisions, recounts, public records, and legal
certification. This paper introduces RCOUNT, a reproducible package format and
verifier contract for replaying election-count claims from public evidence.
RCOUNT does not certify elections and does not prove voter choices. Instead, it
binds normalized contest summaries, reporting units, batches, lineage events,
source hashes, and privacy-safe proof sketches into a package that third parties
can verify mechanically. We describe the package layers, the core reconciliation
checks, the optional RPLAN boundary for district aggregation, and a synthetic
fixture suite that includes both positive and negative multi-election cases.
\end{abstract}
