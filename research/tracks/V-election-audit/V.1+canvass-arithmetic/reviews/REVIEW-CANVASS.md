## Summary

The draft understands the central canvass distinction: election-night returns
are preliminary, certified totals are legal records, and RCOUNT verifies the
public evidence trail between them. The correction-event framing is strong and
does not treat all changed totals as suspicious.

## Major Issues

1. **Status events need a minimal schema.** The text says a correction event has
   authority, source references, explanation, and a transition, but the paper
   should show the fields. Readers need to see what proves "why this total
   changed" in the package.

2. **Jurisdiction variation needs concrete hooks.** The boundaries section says
   workflows vary, but it should name the adapter-level facts that differ:
   cure deadlines, provisional adjudication rules, recount triggers, board
   minutes, court orders, and source artifact names.

3. **Certification language is good but should repeat in the correction
   section.** The paper says RCOUNT does not prove lawfulness. A corrected
   canvass example should also say what a passing event check does and does not
   mean to an election official.

## Minor Issues

- Add a tiny lifecycle diagram or transition table.
- Distinguish "canvassed" from "certified" one more time.
- Name amended certification as a legal event, not just another status.

## Strengths

- Excellent opening distinction: explain change, not merely detect change.
- Good use of positive and negative fixtures.
- The status-specific jurisdiction-total rule is a core contribution.
