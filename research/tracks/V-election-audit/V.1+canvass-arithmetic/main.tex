\begin{abstract}
Election totals change for legitimate reasons between election night and
certification: late mail ballots arrive, provisional ballots are adjudicated,
write-ins are reviewed, duplicates are resolved, and recounts or court orders
may alter the public record. This paper describes how RCOUNT represents that
evolution without treating every change as either fraud or final truth. We
define canvass arithmetic as the collection of status-specific summary checks,
jurisdiction rollups, public status events, batch accounting equations, and
source hash checks that let third parties replay public count claims. Using the
\texttt{canvass-correction}, \texttt{mail-batch-added}, and
\texttt{missing-batch} synthetic fixtures, we show how a verifier can identify
the layer where a canvass claim passes or fails while leaving legal
certification to election officials and courts.
\end{abstract}
