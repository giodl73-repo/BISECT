\section{Batch Manifests}

Batch manifests make the hashing and accounting surface smaller than the whole
election. A package can describe election-day batches, mail batches,
provisional batches, or central-count batches and bind each manifest to source
references.

In \texttt{mail-batch-added}, the batch ledger contains:

\begin{center}
\small
\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{lrrrX}
\toprule
Batch & Accepted & Counted & Rejected & Source reference \\
\midrule
P-001 election day & 70 & 70 & 0 & \texttt{synthetic-summary-export} \\
P-001 late mail & 10 & 10 & 0 & \texttt{synthetic-summary-export} \\
P-002 election day & 60 & 60 & 0 & \texttt{synthetic-summary-export} \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}
\end{center}

The manifest gives RCOUNT two kinds of evidence. The arithmetic evidence checks
that accepted ballots equal counted plus rejected ballots. The source evidence
checks that the public input bytes referenced by the batch still match the
source index. A late mail batch is therefore not treated as suspicious because
it is late; it is auditable because it is declared, reconciled, and source-bound.
