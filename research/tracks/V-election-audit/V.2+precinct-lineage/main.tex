\begin{abstract}
Election comparisons often assume that a precinct identifier means the same
thing across cycles. That assumption fails when precincts split, merge, rename,
or move under new reporting conventions. This paper describes how RCOUNT treats
reporting-unit lineage as public evidence. A lineage event links prior-cycle
units to current-cycle units, records the authority and explanation for the
change, and lets multi-election verification distinguish real administrative
change from stale plans, broken crosswalks, or tampered sources. Using the
\texttt{precinct-split-lineage} fixture and the three-cycle synthetic harness,
we show how RCOUNT can compare elections without reducing precinct history to
fragile string matching.
\end{abstract}
