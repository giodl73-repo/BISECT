\section{Split And Merge Fixture}

The \texttt{precinct-split-lineage} fixture contains two canonical events:

\begin{center}
\small
\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{lXX}
\toprule
Event & Prior units & Current units \\
\midrule
\texttt{lineage:P-004-split} & \texttt{P-004} & \texttt{P-004A}, \texttt{P-004B} \\
\texttt{lineage:P-007-P-008-merge} & \texttt{P-007}, \texttt{P-008} & \texttt{P-078} \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}
\end{center}

The first event says that P-004 split into two precincts after municipal
growth. The second says that P-007 and P-008 merged into P-078 during precinct
consolidation. The verifier's \texttt{lineage\_conservation} check confirms the
cardinality and existence of the referenced units.

\begin{center}
\small
\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{lcccX}
\toprule
Lineage & 2024 unit(s) & Relation & 2028 unit(s) & Meaning \\
\midrule
P-004 split & P-004 & $\rightarrow$ & P-004A + P-004B & One prior unit becomes two current units. \\
P-007/P-008 merge & P-007 + P-008 & $\rightarrow$ & P-078 & Two prior units become one current unit. \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}
\end{center}

The fixture is intentionally about evidence shape, not demographic judgment. It
does not claim that a split is harmless or that a merge is fair. It gives later
analysis a stable, auditable way to avoid comparing P-004 in one cycle to only
P-004A in the next.
