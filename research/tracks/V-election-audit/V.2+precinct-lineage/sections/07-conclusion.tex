\section{Conclusion}

Precinct lineage makes cross-election count verification honest about
administrative change. Instead of pretending that labels are stable, RCOUNT
records split and merge evidence, verifies referenced units, and binds
multi-cycle aggregation to the count package, plan hash, and unit universe used
in each cycle.

This is the foundation for later V-track work. Tamper-evident batch hashing,
CVR reconciliation, and district vote aggregation all depend on knowing which
public units existed when, how they changed, and which source evidence supports
that history.
