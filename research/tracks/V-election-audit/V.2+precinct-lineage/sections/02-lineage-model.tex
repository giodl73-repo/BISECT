\section{Lineage Model}

An RCOUNT lineage event relates reporting units across cycles. The core fields
are:

\begin{center}
\small
\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{lX}
\toprule
Field & Meaning \\
\midrule
\texttt{lineage\_id} & Stable event identifier within the package. \\
\texttt{kind} & Administrative relation: unchanged, split, or merge in the current draft. \\
\texttt{prior\_cycle}, \texttt{current\_cycle} & Election cycles connected by the event. \\
\texttt{prior\_reporting\_unit\_ids} & Units from the earlier cycle. \\
\texttt{current\_reporting\_unit\_ids} & Units in the later cycle. \\
\texttt{authority} & Public office, board, or source asserting the change. \\
\texttt{explanation} & Human-readable reason, such as growth, consolidation, or redrawn precinct boundaries. \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}
\end{center}

The verifier checks structure before interpretation. A split must name one prior
unit and multiple current units. A merge must name multiple prior units and one
current unit. Every referenced unit must exist in the package's reporting-unit
records. Those checks do not prove that the county drew the new units correctly;
they prove that the declared cross-cycle relationship is internally replayable.

Lineage is distinct from count totals. The lineage event says how units relate;
the summaries still carry contest, status, residual, and counted-ballot
arithmetic. This separation keeps an administrative crosswalk from silently
rewriting votes.
