\section{Introduction}

Precincts are administrative objects, not eternal atoms. A county may split a
precinct after growth, merge two precincts after consolidation, rename a unit,
or report vote-center and central-count batches differently from one cycle to
the next. A public audit system that compares elections by matching raw precinct
labels will eventually confuse administrative change with electoral change.

V.1 described how RCOUNT keeps canvass statuses from overwriting one another.
V.2 extends that idea across election cycles. The unit universe for one election
is allowed to differ from the next, but the difference must be declared through
lineage evidence that a verifier can replay.

The goal is modest and important. RCOUNT does not decide whether a precinct
change was wise, lawful, or politically neutral. It verifies that the package
declares how prior reporting units relate to current reporting units, that the
referenced units exist, that each cycle's sources still hash correctly, and
that any RPLAN district aggregation binds the exact unit universe used in that
cycle.
