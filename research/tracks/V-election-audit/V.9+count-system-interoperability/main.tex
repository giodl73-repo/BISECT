\begin{abstract}
Election results are published through many local workflows: election-night
exports, statements of votes, canvass reports, vendor reports, spreadsheets,
PDFs, and common-data-format files. RCOUNT should not pretend those sources are
already neutral ledgers. It should make adapter work explicit. This paper
defines the first V.9 interoperability slices: a statement-of-votes CSV adapter
and a small NIST Election Results Reporting CDF-style JSON adapter. Both
normalize contest, reporting-unit, selection, residual, status, and
jurisdiction-total evidence into ordinary RCOUNT packages while preserving the
source file hash. The adapters are intentionally small, but they establish the
contract future jurisdiction-specific adapters should follow: parse, normalize,
hash the original source, verify the resulting package, and label any claim the
adapter cannot make.
\end{abstract}
