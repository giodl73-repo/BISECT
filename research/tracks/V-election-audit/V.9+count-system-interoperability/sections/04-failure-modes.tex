\section{Failure Modes}

The adapter rejects missing required cells, bad status labels, unsupported
reporting-unit kinds, invalid numeric fields, duplicate selection rows inside a
summary, and conflicting repeated metadata. After import, the ordinary verifier
catches the deeper count errors: selection sums that do not equal counted
ballots, jurisdiction totals that do not equal precinct totals, unknown
selections, negative counts, and source-file tampering.

This two-layer structure matters. Parser failures are evidence-meaning failures:
the source cannot be safely normalized. Verifier failures are package-consistency
failures: the source was normalized, but its declared equations do not hold.
Keeping those categories separate makes later vendor adapters easier to audit.
