## Module Score: 7.8/10

## Track-Level Strengths
- O.1 is the track's strongest paper: the 40% vs 17% competitive district finding is striking and policy-relevant. The comparison to Iowa (44% competitive) is the right benchmark.
- O.4's constituent distance analysis is methodologically clean — geographic distance is directly computable, requires no causal identification, and the r=-0.67 PP correlation is strong.
- O.5's RQI legal testimony paragraph is the most practitioner-useful single passage in the entire O track. "A plan at RQI < 0.50 fails to deliver median-quality democratic representation" is court-admissible language.

## P1 Items by Paper
- **O.2**: 20-observation dataset (5 states × 4 cycles) is underpowered. The standard error on the turnout coefficient (0.61) is almost as large as the point estimate (1.42) in the key specification. Must note limited statistical power clearly in abstract.
- **O.3**: The counterfactual projection uses the within-member PP slope applied between-plan — this extrapolation is explicitly labeled preliminary but needs a formal statement of the identifying assumption and its limitations in the main text (not just conclusion).
- **O.5**: The Pew Research correlation (r=0.54) is over 18 states — the paper should report the states included and note whether the RQI variation in the sample is sufficient to support the correlation claim.

## P2 Items
- O.1: Add a comparison to Iowa (commission redistricting) — Iowa's 44% competitive district fraction is the established benchmark for neutral redistricting. Bisect at 40% is close but not identical. (Jacobson)
- O.2: Extend to county-level analysis if county-level turnout data is available — 50 observations would substantially improve power. (Caughey)
- O.3: Consider an instrumental variable using redistricting cycle boundaries as the instrument for compactness change — this is the obvious identification strategy and should be mentioned as future work. (Rodden)
- O.5: Equal-weight justification beyond "avoids criticism of cherry-picking" — cite the political science literature on composite index weighting choices. (Stephanopoulos)

## Next Action
O.2 and O.3 P1 items (statistical power disclosure and extrapolation assumption statement) are the most critical — both are prose fixes that will satisfy the reviewers without additional data collection. O.5 Pew validation documentation is a one-sentence table addition.
