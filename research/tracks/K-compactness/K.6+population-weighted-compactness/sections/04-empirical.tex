\subsection{Key Observations}

\paragraph{Prime-factor leads PWC performance.}
On NC 2020, prime-factor achieves mean PWC $= 62.1^\dagger$ km$^2$,
a 14\% reduction from standard-bisect ($72.1^\dagger$ km$^2$).
This confirms Claim~2: hierarchical nesting in prime-factor aligns population
concentrations with district centroids.

\paragraph{TX high variance.}
TX exhibits a mean-to-minimum ratio of $124.7 / 18.4 \approx 6.8$ for standard-bisect,
much higher than NC ($72.1 / 24.3 \approx 3.0$) and WI ($55.8 / 19.2 \approx 2.9$).
This reflects TX's geographic heterogeneity: dense urban Dallas-Fort Worth
tracts contribute low-PWC districts while vast West Texas districts contribute
high-PWC districts.
Variance of PWC across TX districts is $> 3\times$ higher than NC or WI.

\paragraph{WI low variance.}
WI shows the most uniform PWC across districts, consistent with its more
spatially dispersed population pattern (no dominant megacity; mid-sized
Milwaukee, Madison, Green Bay).
