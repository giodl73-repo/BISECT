Computing the minimum bounding circle (MBC) of a polygon is equivalent to
computing the smallest enclosing disk (SED) of its vertex set.
We use Welzl's randomised algorithm~\citep{welzl1991smallest}, which runs in
$O(n)$ expected time.

\subsection{Algorithm}

\begin{algorithm}
\caption{Welzl's Minimum Enclosing Circle}
\label{alg:welzl}
\begin{algorithmic}[1]
\Function{Welzl}{$P$: point set, $R$: boundary set}
  \If{$P = \emptyset$ or $|R| = 3$}
    \State \Return \Call{MinCircleFromBoundary}{$R$}
  \EndIf
  \State Choose $p \in P$ uniformly at random
  \State $D \gets$ \Call{Welzl}{$P \setminus \{p\}$, $R$}
  \If{$p \in D$}
    \State \Return $D$
  \EndIf
  \State \Return \Call{Welzl}{$P \setminus \{p\}$, $R \cup \{p\}$}
\EndFunction
\end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}

\texttt{MinCircleFromBoundary($R$)} returns the unique circle defined by
$|R|$ points: the trivial circle for $|R| = 0$ (radius 0), the circle with
diameter $\overline{p_1 p_2}$ for $|R| = 2$, and the circumcircle of
$\{p_1, p_2, p_3\}$ for $|R| = 3$.

\begin{theorem}[Welzl's correctness]
  Algorithm~\ref{alg:welzl} returns the unique minimum enclosing circle
  of $P$, and runs in $O(n)$ expected time.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
Uniqueness of the MBC follows from strict convexity of the containment constraint
(the MBC is the unique solution to a strictly convex program).
The $O(n)$ expected time bound follows from the backward analysis of the
randomised algorithm~\citep{welzl1991smallest}: the probability that a
newly added point $p$ is on the boundary of the MBC is at most $3/i$ when
$|P| = i$, giving the harmonic series $\sum_{i=1}^{n} O(3/i) = O(n)$. \qed
\end{proof}

\subsection{Implementation}

The implementation in \texttt{crates/bisect-analysis/src/compactness.rs}
extracts all polygon vertices (including holes), shuffles them using the
run's seed for reproducibility (seed $= 42^\dagger$), and calls Welzl's algorithm.
Test invariants (L0):
\begin{itemize}
  \item Reock of a circle is $1.0$ (exact to floating-point precision).
  \item Welzl MBC contains all input points: for all $p \in P$,
    $\|p - \text{centre}\| \leq r + 10^{-9}$.
  \item MBC area $\geq$ polygon area for any convex polygon.
\end{itemize}
