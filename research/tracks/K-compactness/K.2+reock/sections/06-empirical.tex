\subsection{Key Observations}

\paragraph{WI consistently outperforms NC and TX.}
Wisconsin's compact state shape produces higher Reock values across all algorithms.
The WI moving-knife mean Reock of $0.48^\dagger$ is 26\% higher than the
corresponding NC value ($0.38^\dagger$), reflecting the absence of large coastal
appendages and Texas's geographic scale constraints.

\paragraph{MKA advantage is stable.}
MKA outperforms standard-bisect by $0.07$ (mean Reock) across all three states---a
consistent margin that validates MKA's design objective.

\paragraph{TX shows greatest need for Reock.}
TX has the lowest Reock values across all algorithms due to the combination of
large district count ($k=38$), diverse geography (coastal Gulf, arid West, urban
Dallas-Fort Worth), and the geometric challenges of fitting 38 districts into
a large non-convex state.
The TX minimum district Reock under standard-bisect is $0.18^\dagger$---the
``worst'' district across all algorithm-state pairs---indicating districts that
fill only 18\% of their MBC.
