\subsection{Key Results}

\paragraph{All bisect algorithms satisfy LW $\leq 2.8$.}
The maximum LW across all 12 algorithm-state combinations is $2.79^\dagger$
(TX standard-bisect), comfortably below the LW $> 3$ court threshold.
Moving-knife consistently produces the lowest maximum LW across all states.

\paragraph{NC under moving-knife.}
NC moving-knife achieves maximum LW $= 2.38^\dagger$, the best performance
for NC. This reflects MKA's design: by maximising minimum Reock, it also
controls elongation, since round (high Reock) districts tend to have low LW.

\paragraph{Zero districts exceed the threshold.}
Across all $4 \times 3 = 12$ algorithm-state combinations, zero districts
exceed LW $= 3.0$. This is a strong result for litigation: a practitioner
can certify that no bisect-generated district is ``elongated'' by the
court-applied standard.
