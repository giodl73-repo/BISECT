\section{Conclusion}

T.15 adds clustering as a first-class construction family while preserving the
audit fixed point: final plans must converge into RPLAN, RCTX, audit
certificates, and manifests. The contribution is the inspectable capacity and
repair contract, not a claim that clustered plans are globally optimal,
politically fair, or legally sufficient.

That contract makes T.15 a useful companion to spectral, regionalization, and
flow construction. It gives future comparison papers a baseline that can say
not only what plan was produced, but also whether capacity pressure caused
repair, infeasibility, or a clean final export.
