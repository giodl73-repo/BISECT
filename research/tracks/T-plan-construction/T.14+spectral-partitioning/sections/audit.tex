\section{Audit Artifacts}

When spectral construction is exported as a final plan, the paper follows the
shared RPLAN and RCTX fixed point: assignments, algorithm lineage, parameter
hashes, and validation results are written as sidecars that can be checked by
the verifier without re-running the constructor. The audit claim is limited to
the declared profile and does not imply political fairness or legal compliance.
The audit package also does not prove that the spectral cut is unique,
globally optimal, or preferable to another valid construction family.

For a reader inspecting an exported package, the fixed point is the pair
``declared method identity plus replayable sidecars.'' The certificate can say
that the plan was emitted under the spectral profile with the recorded
parameters and validation checks. It cannot say that the graph coordinate was
the mathematically exact Fiedler vector, that the districting outcome satisfies
state-specific legal standards, or that another construction family would not
produce a better plan under some external objective.
