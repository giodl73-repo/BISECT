\section{Introduction}

Spectral graph partitioning is a natural deterministic baseline for
redistricting because it exposes a continuous relaxation of the cut problem.
This paper positions T.14 as a transparent construction method whose value is
explainability, reproducibility, and comparison against stronger construction
and optimization families.

The phrase ``spectral'' is used carefully here. The implemented T.14 slice is a
deterministic spectral-style bisection baseline: it builds a graph coordinate
by smoothing an anchored vector over adjacency, sorts units by that coordinate,
and then selects a population-feasible sweep cut. It is not yet a full Lanczos
or production eigensolver implementation of the exact Fiedler vector. That
distinction is part of the contribution boundary rather than an implementation
detail, because it tells the reader which claims are supported today: replayable
construction, fixture behavior, and audit lineage, not spectral optimality.
