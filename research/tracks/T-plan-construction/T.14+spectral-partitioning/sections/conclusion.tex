\section{Conclusion}

T.14 adds a cheap, deterministic construction baseline to the BISECT family.
Its publication value is clearest when it is presented as an auditable point of
comparison rather than as the strongest available optimizer. The fixed-point
audit contract makes the output reproducible under a declared profile, while
the evidence ladder keeps fixture correctness, sidecar validation, and future
empirical quality claims separate.

The next scientific step is comparative rather than rhetorical: run the same
states, tolerances, and audit profiles across spectral, METIS, GeoSection,
capacity clustering, regionalization, and flow construction, then publish only
the differences supported by recorded commands and packages.
