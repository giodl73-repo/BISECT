\section{Limitations}

Spectral partitioning is a relaxation. A valid and deterministic spectral plan
does not imply global optimality, political fairness, or legal compliance beyond
the declared audit profile.

The current implementation is also a staged spectral-style baseline rather than
a full eigensolver. Its smoothing coordinate is useful because it is cheap,
stable, and auditable, but claims that depend on exact Laplacian eigenvectors
remain future work.

The method is also sensitive to graph construction choices. Disconnected
components, weakly connected geography, nearly symmetric graphs, and edge
weight conventions can change the spectral coordinate and therefore the sweep
order. Those are inspectable inputs to the audit trail, but they are not solved
by determinism alone.
