\begin{abstract}
This draft studies spectral graph partitioning as a deterministic construction
baseline for congressional redistricting. The paper focuses on the staged T.14
implementation: Fiedler-vector approximation, population-balanced sweep cuts,
recursive proportional target handling, and RPLAN audit sidecars through the
BISECT CLI. The central claim is modest: spectral partitioning is a transparent
baseline for comparison, not an exact optimizer or a replacement for
METIS-family construction.
\end{abstract}
