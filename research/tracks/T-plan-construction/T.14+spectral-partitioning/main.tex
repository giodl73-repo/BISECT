\begin{abstract}
This paper studies spectral-style graph partitioning as a deterministic construction
baseline for congressional redistricting. The paper focuses on the staged T.14
implementation: smoothed graph-coordinate approximation, population-balanced sweep cuts,
recursive proportional target handling, and RPLAN audit sidecars through the
BISECT CLI. The central claim is modest: spectral partitioning is a transparent
deterministic baseline for comparison. RPLAN/RCTX sidecars verify declared
profile and lineage, not fairness, legal compliance, exact optimality, or a
replacement for METIS-family construction.
\end{abstract}
