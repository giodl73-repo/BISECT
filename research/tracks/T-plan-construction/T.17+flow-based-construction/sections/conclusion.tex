\section{Conclusion}

T.17 brings flow construction into the BISECT algorithm family while preserving
the shared final-plan audit path used across the T/U roadmap. Its role is a
model-relative, auditable construction baseline; stronger exact optimization
claims belong to the later U.16 and U.17 papers.

The immediate contribution is therefore the witness contract: a flow-family
constructor can say what bounds, seeds, costs, repair mode, and status produced
the artifact. That makes later exact solvers easier to compare without
overstating what this first construction slice has proven.
