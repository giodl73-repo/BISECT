\section{Evaluation Plan}

Evidence includes RPLAN crate tests, certificate-verifier tests, BISECT bridge
tests, final-plan sidecars across construction, exact, local-search, and Pareto
families, public golden packages, a U.20 grid10 audit benchmark package, and a
tamper/failure-mode catalog.

The evaluation standard is therefore mechanical rather than rhetorical. A
method earns the right to appear in downstream reports only after its exported
package survives manifest checks, certificate checks, and the negative fixture
catalog. If a package lacks context, mutates a plan, changes a context hash,
changes unit order, reuses the wrong profile, or smuggles reserved lineage
fields into method metadata, the verifier must reject it with a specific reason.
