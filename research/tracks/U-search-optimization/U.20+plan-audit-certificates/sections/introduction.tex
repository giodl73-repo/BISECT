\section{Introduction}

U.20 documents \texttt{rplan-core}, \texttt{rplan-io}, \texttt{rplan-audit},
\texttt{rplan-cli}, and the BISECT manifest verifier. The paper asks whether
BISECT can use a neutral plan/context/certificate family as the fixed point for
construction, exact, local-search, Pareto, and future algorithm families.

The fixed point is not another search method. It is the acceptance contract at
the end of a method run. A construction algorithm may export a deterministic
baseline package, an exact method may export a solve report and final plan, a
large-neighborhood search may export a parent/child improvement record, and a
Pareto search may export a selected frontier row. U.20 says what all of those
outputs must become before they are carried into reports: a plan, a context, a
profile-scoped certificate, a manifest, and a lineage record with stable hashes.

This narrowness is the core design choice. RPLAN/RCTX does not erase the
method-specific evidence from T.14--T.17 or U.16--U.19. Instead, it gives those
families one common final-plan surface so that BISECT can verify package
integrity, context agreement, declared profile checks, and provenance without
pretending to re-prove every upstream algorithmic claim.
