\section{Conclusion}

U.20 is the fixed-point paper for the roadmap. It explains how heterogeneous
algorithm families can converge on a common plan/context/audit package while
keeping their method-specific evidence and limitations explicit. That closes
the T/U implementation arc in a precise way: construction, exact optimization,
local search, and Pareto selection may disagree about how candidate plans are
found, but they now agree on what a final plan must carry before BISECT treats
it as auditable.

The invariant is intentionally narrow. Certificates are useful because they are
profile-scoped and tied to verifiable package semantics rather than broad legal
or political conclusions. U.20 therefore turns the algorithm family into an
inspectable artifact pipeline: method evidence first, fixed-point package next,
downstream report last.
