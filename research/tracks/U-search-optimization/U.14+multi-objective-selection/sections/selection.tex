\section{Selection Framework}

Selection should record objective definitions, weights or dominance rules,
frontier position, candidate set provenance, and the reason a final plan was
chosen. The goal is transparency, not automatic neutrality.

\begin{table}[htbp]
\centering
\small
\begin{tabular}{p{0.24\linewidth}p{0.32\linewidth}p{0.28\linewidth}}
\toprule
Selection input & Required record & Main risk if omitted \\
\midrule
Objective vector & Metric definitions and computation command & Unclear trade-off meaning \\
Candidate set & Generator, seed, filters, and count & Cherry-picking ambiguity \\
Dominance rule & Pareto, weighted score, threshold, or tie-break & Hidden policy choice \\
Chosen plan & Selection rationale and rank/frontier position & False neutrality claim \\
Export package & RPLAN/RCTX plus objective sidecars & Unreproducible final plan \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\caption{Minimum record for auditable multi-objective selection.}
\end{table}

The selected plan should therefore be accompanied by the rejected context. When
only the final assignment is exported, readers cannot tell whether the plan was
representative of the frontier, an extreme point, a compromise under declared
weights, or a policy choice after inspection.
