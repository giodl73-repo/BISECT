\section{Introduction}

U.19 documents \texttt{bisect-pareto} and \texttt{bisect-cli::pareto\_cmd},
exposed through \texttt{bisect pareto} and selected-frontier audit flags. The
paper is an evolutionary/multi-objective implementation paper, not a proof that
a computed frontier is globally complete.

The method belongs beside U.14 and U.15 rather than replacing them. U.19 can
show that a frontier was generated under a seed, budget, and objective vector;
U.14 supplies the selection record explaining why one non-dominated entry was
chosen; U.15 states the legal or policy posture that may be attached to that
choice. Once a frontier entry is selected, U.20's fixed-point audit path takes
over: the selected plan must be exported as RPLAN/RCTX with certificate and
manifest sidecars.
