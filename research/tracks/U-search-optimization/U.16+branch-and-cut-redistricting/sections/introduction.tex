\section{Introduction}

U.16 documents the \texttt{bisect-ilp} and \texttt{bisect-cli}
branch-and-cut surface:
\texttt{--structure ilp --ilp-method branch-and-cut}. Its claim is not that
every redistricting instance is solved exactly at scale, but that
exact-optimization runs should expose formulation, separation, proof status,
and audit lineage in a reproducible form.

The paper sits between the algorithm-selection vocabulary of U.13 and the
fixed-point audit contract of U.20. U.13 asks when an exact method is the right
tool; U.16 says what evidence an exact branch-and-cut run must emit before it
is allowed to use exact language; U.20 explains how the resulting plan and
context become a stable auditable object. The central distinction is that an
RPLAN/RCTX certificate can show that a final plan is valid and traceable, while
only a solver report can show that a particular model was solved, bounded, or
left unresolved.
