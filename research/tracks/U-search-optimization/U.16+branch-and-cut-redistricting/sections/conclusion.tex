\section{Conclusion}

U.16 turns branch-and-cut from a black-box exact option into an auditable
implementation contract. Its contribution is the separation of three claims:
the ILP formulation can be rebuilt, the separation loop can report violated
connectivity cuts and proof status, and the final plan can be certified as an
RPLAN/RCTX fixed point.

Once those semantics are stable, branch-and-cut can sit beside heuristic and
construction methods without category confusion: exact claims live in the solver
report, validity and lineage claims live in the audit package, and future
performance claims live in benchmark evidence.
