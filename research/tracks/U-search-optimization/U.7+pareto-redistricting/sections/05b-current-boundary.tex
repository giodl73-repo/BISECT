\section{Current Implementation and Evidence Boundary}

The current \texttt{bisect-pareto} crate implements NSGA-II frontier generation
and selected-frontier packages with RPLAN/RCTX sidecars.  That supports an
auditable selected-frontier record, not a guarantee that the sampled frontier is
the complete mathematical Pareto frontier.  ``Pareto-optimal'' in this context
means non-dominated within the generated candidate population unless an exact
certificate says otherwise.

Legal posture claims such as enacted-plan dominance require the full candidate
set definition, objective functions, selected frontier index, audit certificate,
and verifier result.  Without those artifacts, the paper should be read as a
method and selection-record design rather than evidence about a particular
state's enacted plan.
