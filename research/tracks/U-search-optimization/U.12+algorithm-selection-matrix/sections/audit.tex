\section{Audit Boundary}

The RPLAN/RCTX fixed point is the common handoff. Selection guidance should say
which sidecars, manifests, certificates, or run summaries are required before a
workflow can support a stated claim.

The audit boundary also prevents category mistakes. A certificate can show that
a declared verifier accepted a plan package, or that an exact method reported a
bound under stated assumptions. It does not make an ensemble representative,
make a heuristic globally optimal, or make a legal posture sufficient outside
the recorded profile.

For that reason, the selection record should travel with the package. A reader
should be able to inspect the RPLAN/RCTX identifiers, the recipe hash, the
sidecar list, the method report or certificate path, and a one-sentence claim
boundary. If any of those fields are missing, the workflow may still be useful
for exploration, but it is not ready to support a publication or litigation
claim.
