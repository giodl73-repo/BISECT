# U.15 - Legal Postures for Search

**Paper Type:** Legal/policy interpretation  
**Status:** Planning  
**Track:** U - Search and Optimization  
**Code Home:** reports, manifests, audit certificates, search outputs

## Research Question

How should search and optimization outputs be framed in legal or policy settings
without overstating neutrality, fairness, or compliance?

## Hypotheses / Claims

- **H1:** Different search methods support different legal postures: baseline, counterexample, robustness, outlier, or certificate.
- **H2:** Explicit claim discipline reduces the risk of treating algorithmic validity as legal neutrality.
- **H3:** RPLAN audit artifacts improve transparency while preserving the limits of legal interpretation.

Falsification: examples where the same output supports incompatible legal claims
without additional assumptions.

## Scope Boundary

- **In scope:** claim categories, examples, limitations, audit-language guidance.
- **Out of scope:** jurisdiction-specific legal advice or broad claims of compliance.
- **Generalizability claim:** this is guidance for BISECT reports and papers.

## Evaluation Plan

- Baselines: generic "algorithmic map is fair" language.
- Evidence: U.20 audit caveats, report language, legal specs.
- Success criteria: each posture has supported claims and prohibited claims.

## Figures and Tables

- Legal posture matrix.
- Output-to-claim mapping.
- Overclaim examples and corrected wording.

## Limitations

- Legal standards evolve and differ by jurisdiction.
- Expert legal review is still required before real litigation use.
