\section{Introduction}

U.13 is a methodology and claim-discipline paper. It distinguishes exact
optimization certificates from heuristic search evidence and from RPLAN/RCTX
audit validation.

The distinction matters because the U-series deliberately mixes exact and
heuristic work. Branch-and-cut and branch-and-price papers may eventually
produce bounds or infeasibility witnesses for scoped formulations. LNS,
evolutionary, ensemble, and Pareto workflows can produce useful plans, traces,
and comparisons. Audit certificates can make either lineage inspectable. These
outputs should meet at the fixed point without being described as the same kind
of proof.
