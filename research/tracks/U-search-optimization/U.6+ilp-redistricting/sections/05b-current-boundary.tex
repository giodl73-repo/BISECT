\section{Current Implementation and Evidence Boundary}

Exact optimization claims require an explicit solver certificate.  The current
\texttt{bisect-ilp} crate contains formulation summaries, solver-result and
certificate types, and tests for the exact-method boundary; full MPS/LP export
and external solver replay are separate evidence layers that must be present
before claiming a courtroom-grade optimum for a real state.

Accordingly, this paper should distinguish three statuses: formulation-only,
bounded solve, and proven optimum.  A proven optimum must name the model,
instance hash, solver and version, objective, gap tolerance, incumbent, bound,
and verifier path.  For larger states, ILP remains a benchmark or subproblem
tool unless the archived package proves otherwise.
