\section{Current Implementation and Evidence Boundary}

Adaptive multiscale search has a current Rust substrate in
\texttt{bisect-multiscale}, including Robbins--Monro-style tuning parameters.
The evidence boundary remains narrower than the mathematical convergence
language: adaptation can stabilize a tuning trace under a declared target, but
that does not prove representative sampling or legal sufficiency of the selected
plan.

Published claims should name the adaptation schedule, target acceptance rate,
resolution hierarchy, seed, diagnostics, and package hashes.  Without those
artifacts, empirical statements about state-specific optimal $\alpha$ values
should be treated as calibration examples rather than final benchmark results.
