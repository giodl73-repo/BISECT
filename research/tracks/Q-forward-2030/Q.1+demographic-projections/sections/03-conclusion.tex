\section{Conclusion}

The 2030 congressional map will be substantially reshaped by Sun Belt growth
and Rust Belt/Northeast decline.
The eight projected gainers are overwhelmingly in the South and Mountain West;
the ten losers are in the industrial Midwest and Northeast.

For the bisect pipeline, the critical 2030 planning items are:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Pre-build bisection trees for all 18 states with projected seat changes,
    including both the medium-series baseline and the $\pm1$ scenario for
    uncertain states.
  \item Validate TX $k=41$ prime fallback (20+21 binary split) via a test run
    before Census data arrives.
  \item Validate FL $k=30$ prime factorization tree ($2\times3\times5$).
  \item Pre-register the algorithm version (bisect v0.2.0 or successor) to
    satisfy the DIA's statutory seed formula requirement before April 2031.
\end{enumerate}

The projection uncertainty is largest for CA ($-1$ vs.\ hold) and MT ($-1$
vs.\ hold). Both states are within a few thousand people of the seat boundary.
The Census Bureau's 2025 and 2026 annual estimates (released annually) will
progressively narrow the uncertainty as the decade progresses.
