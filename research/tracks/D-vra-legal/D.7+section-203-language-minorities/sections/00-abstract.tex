Section~203 of the Voting Rights Act, codified at 52~U.S.C.~\S~10503,
requires jurisdictions with large language-minority populations to provide
translated election materials---ballots, voter registration forms, and
polling-place instructions---when a language-minority group exceeds 5\%
of voting-age citizens or 10,000 voting-age citizens and the group's
English literacy rate falls below the national average. The 2023 coverage
determination (88 Fed.\ Reg.\ 34,396) covers 331 jurisdictions in 31 states,
with Spanish, Chinese, Filipino, Vietnamese, Korean, and numerous Native
American and Alaska Native languages among those triggering coverage.

Redistricting directly affects Section~203 implementation: when district
boundaries split a Section~203-covered county between two or more congressional
districts, election administrators face increased coordination burden because
each district may require different language materials, with county-level
administration having to serve multiple federal election jurisdictions
simultaneously. This paper analyzes the interaction between Section~203
coverage and congressional redistricting, with particular focus on how
\textsc{bisect} algorithmic redistricting---which minimizes unnecessary
county splits through its graph-partitioning structure---reduces Section~203
administrative fragmentation relative to enacted maps.

Empirical results for Texas (Spanish) and California (multiple languages)
show that \textsc{bisect} county-weight plans achieve a 35\% reduction in
Section~203 community splits in Texas and a 9\% improvement in language
minority community integrity in California relative to the enacted 2022
congressional maps. For Vietnamese-majority tracts in California's Santa
Clara County, \textsc{bisect} preserves community cohesion in 3 of 4
tracts within the same district, compared with 1 of 4 under the enacted map.
We also analyze the interaction between Section~203 coverage and Section~2
majority-minority district requirements, finding that 23 counties nationally
have both Section~203 coverage and Section~2 Gingles feasibility, requiring
the \textsc{bisect} VRA mode (\texttt{--weights-override vra-aligned}) to
satisfy both requirements simultaneously.
