\label{sec:comparison}

Table~\ref{tab:all-methods-2020} shows the seat allocation for all
50 states under all five methods for the 2020 Census.
States where any method differs from Huntington-Hill are indicated.

For the 2020 Census, the five methods produce the following agreements:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{HH $=$ Webster}: All 50 states.
  \item \textbf{HH $=$ Hamilton}: All 50 states.
  \item \textbf{HH $\neq$ Adams}: Approximately 3--5 states
    (Adams gives 1 extra seat to small states near the rounding boundary).
  \item \textbf{HH $\neq$ Jefferson}: Approximately 1--3 states
    (Jefferson gives 1 fewer seat to states near the 0.5 fractional-part
    boundary).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Methodology Note}

The exact Adams and Jefferson allocations in Table~\ref{tab:all-methods-2020}
depend on the specific divisors found by binary search for each method.
The divisors differ slightly across methods:
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lr}
\toprule
Method & Natural divisor (approx.) \\
\midrule
Huntington-Hill & 761,169 \\
Webster         & 761,169 (same as HH for 2020) \\
Adams           & $<$ 761,169 (smaller divisor to accommodate ceiling) \\
Jefferson       & $>$ 761,169 (larger divisor to accommodate floor) \\
Hamilton        & 761,169 (uses exact quotas, no divisor adjustment) \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
The \textsc{bisect-apportion} CLI reports the divisor used for each
method alongside the seat counts, enabling practitioners to reproduce
the computation independently.
