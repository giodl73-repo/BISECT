\begin{abstract}
\noindent
The most frequently asked question about any redistricting algorithm is:
does it systematically favor one party?  This paper frames how to answer that
question by situating the algorithm's plans within partisan outcome
distributions of \recom{} ensembles.  The claim to support with archived traces
is whether \ar{} is systematically biased or instead falls near the ensemble
median/corridor for the tested states.  We characterise the ``proportionality
corridor'' (the fraction of ensemble plans within one seat of proportional) and
explain why algorithm choice
determines which tail you land in, connecting the partisan outcome to B.0's
bakeoff of competing redistricting algorithms.
\end{abstract}
