\begin{abstract}
\noindent
Short-Burst (G.6) is a meta-algorithm: it runs \(n_{\mathrm{bursts}}\) short
Markov chains and selects the endpoint with the best edge-cut.
The underlying chain type is a parameter.
We implement two new chain variants---\sbf{} and \sbms{}---by composing
Short-Burst with ForestRecom (G.9) and MergeSplit (G.10) chains,
respectively.
This pairing separates compactness optimization (the contribution of the
Short-Burst wrapper) from distributional diagnostics (the contribution of the
underlying chain).  The empirical comparison to archive is whether standard
\sburst{}, \sbf{}, and \sbms{} differ in final edge cut, per-burst acceptance,
and coverage near good plans under equal compute budgets.
We introduce the \emph{per-burst acceptance rate} diagnostic, recorded in
the audit manifest, which quantifies the effective burst length for each
chain type.
The three variants should be cited as implemented only where a checked-in
command, test, and package path expose the named modes.
The current \texttt{G.6-G.12+short-burst-smoke} package verifies the seed-stream
and acceptance-diagnostic mechanics, not production CLI availability.
\end{abstract}
