\section{Current Implementation and Diagnostic Boundary}

Short-burst chains are optimization wrappers over chain primitives.  They can
improve the best observed objective under a fixed compute budget, but restarting
from good states changes the sampling story.  Unless the package carries the
underlying chain, restart schedule, burst length, acceptance logs, selected
state, and diagnostics, short-burst output should be treated as heuristic
improvement rather than a representative ensemble sample.

ForestReCom and Merge-Split components add acceptance accounting and diagnostic
structure, but they do not by themselves make the burst-selected plan a draw
from stationarity.  The paper's claim boundary is therefore best-plan search
plus diagnostics, not distributional correctness.

The checked-in \texttt{G.6-G.12+short-burst-smoke} package hash-binds a
synthetic endpoint and seed-stream ledger.  Its verifier checks chain, forward,
and reverse seed derivation, endpoint restart instead of within-burst-minimum
restart, deterministic selected-endpoint ordering, and per-burst acceptance-rate
arithmetic.  It is a mechanics fixture, not a production command package or
state empirical archive.
