\section{Current Diagnostic Evidence Boundary}

The current \texttt{bisect-ensemble} diagnostics include R-hat, ESS-style
summaries, Hamming autocorrelation, acceptance accounting, and step records.
These are run diagnostics: they can falsify a bad ensemble and support a
monitored summary, but they do not certify stationarity or representative
coverage of the entire redistricting state space.

Any convergence claim should name chain count, steps, burn-in/thinning, proposal
kernel, seed schedule, monitored statistic, threshold, and archived diagnostic
JSON.  For legal or public-facing work, the appropriate posture is ``diagnosed
under declared summaries,'' not ``proved mixed.''
